# Four-way draft comparison — GPT 5.5 vs Opus vs Fable vs 5.6 Sol Ultra (objective)

Purpose: a **descriptive, non-evaluative** map of how the four drafts solve the same story — characterizations, scenes, and staged action — so choices can be redlined by hand. No ranking, no "stronger/weaker." Where a draft is quoted, it's to show a concrete difference, not to endorse it.

Drafts covered: `draft-script-gpt5.5.md`, `draft-script-opus.md`, `draft-script-fable.md`, `draft-script-5.6-sol-ultra.md`. Short labels below are **gpt**, **opus**, **fable**, and **sol-ultra**, respectively. (`glm5.2` remains out of scope here.)

## 0. Register / form (observable, not a judgment)

How each draft writes the *page*, which affects what has to be compressed or converted for a shooting script:

| | Action-line style | Authorial voice in action lines | Dialogue density |
|---|---|---|---|
| **gpt** | lean, sparse, present-tense beats; near-shooting-script | minimal; rarely interprets | high; short exchanges carry scenes |
| **opus** | descriptive, image-forward; some interpretation | occasional ("we watch him decide") | medium; dialogue + atmosphere share load |
| **fable** | long, essayistic; frequently addresses the reader/audience and states intent ("this is the thesis," "the audience holds its breath," "the film keeps its own counsel") | heavy; interprets its own scenes | medium; wrapped in interpretive prose |
| **sol-ultra** | expansive, scene-granular, present-tense screenplay prose; 68 numbered scenes | moderate; sometimes interprets motive or meaning ("not permission. An admission…") | high; sustained exchanges share space with detailed blocking |

Consequence for merging: gpt is the leanest conventional script surface. sol-ultra also uses conventional screenplay action/dialogue but expands most beats into separate scenes and longer exchanges. opus gives more page space to image and atmosphere. fable states intent directly in treatment-like narration, so those passages would need to be cut or converted into playable action.

## 3. Deep dives (the scenes that diverge most)

### 3.1 Cold open / Krem

Same skeleton in all four: ordinary warm family → polite (non-violent) entrance → Krem takes everything → an *off-ramp exists* → he chooses to kill the father as a lesson, eyes on the child. Divergences:

- **What leaves the scene as an object.** gpt/opus carry no comparably specific family object forward. **fable** makes the father's hunting crossbow the murder weapon, tracker, brawl weapon and climax weapon. **sol-ultra** makes the white-gripped pistol the murder weapon, tracked object and recovered family object; in parallel, Ruthye steals Krem's jump-seed here, he retrieves it at the bar, and a Brigand shuttle's donor seed eventually repairs Kara's ship.
- **Weapon of the kill.** gpt: unspecified gun. opus: a short **blade**, kneeling by the pinned father. fable: the hunting crossbow. sol-ultra: the family's white-gripped **field pistol**, fired after Krem has crossed the threshold to leave.
- **How the mirror is planted.** fable names **Bilki** and the **Yellow Hills** in the kitchen and gives Krem an explicit "I was like you." sol-ultra also names **Bilki**, but withholds the Yellow Hills title until the handover; its mirror is visual ("Krem knows that face"), and Ruthye later returns his exact worldview line. gpt/opus keep his origin generic and state the resemblance directly.
- **The mother's blow.** opus and fable explicitly define the backhand as "to see who moves" / "procedure." gpt stages it with less emphasis on the test. sol-ultra lets the apparent crisis end after Father responds, then makes Krem's separate decision at the doorway turn on Ruthye's stare.
- **Family texture.** gpt: Father/Mother/Brother (unnamed). opus: Alder/Nell/Pip. fable: Andy/Marye/Tomme, plus a six-legged pet and market/dance banter. sol-ultra: unnamed Father/Mother and Tavi, with the kite, pie, farm labor, the white pistol, and "a life in your keeping" all recurring after the murder.
- **Local complicity.** fable emphasizes neighbors honoring contracts with Bilki's killers. sol-ultra makes the connection physically local: Krem points to a Bilki-made stove and names Evelie-supplied civilian components repurposed as weapons; Father denies that his family was part of it.

### 3.2 Medbay

Same function (diagnose poison, establish no on-hand cure, decide to run for a yellow sun). The four stage the dread differently:

- **gpt:** shows **numbers** ("not friendly"), then undercuts with a human detail — a **child's drawing of Krypto** taped over Kryptonian instructions ("Someone who thought I answered messages") — and gives Ruthye a **hands-on mechanical task** (the pressure valve; "And if you do not say?" / "Use judgment"), establishing her competence early.
- **opus:** replaces numbers with a **metaphor** — the toxicology readout is a **decay curve**, and Ruthye reads it: "It's not a clock. It's a hill." Kara explains the sun as "a plant that's been in a closet." No drawing, no valve; the weight is in the image.
- **fable:** replaces numbers with **sound** — a medbay **tick** ("a metronome the movie will never let us stop hearing") and "It gives him 'soon.'" Ends on "**why in all the worlds do you live here?**," which Kara cannot answer. Ruthye is "present," not mechanical.
- **sol-ultra:** uses a contracting **amber-to-red viability band**, then puts ship viability and patient viability on the same route chart so neither line reaches the yellow star. The empty/expired cabinet and 417-day maintenance delay make the failed response an extension of Kara's neglect. Ruthye's contribution is decisional (accept safe harbor) rather than medical/mechanical. A second diagnosis at the rest stop corrects the first plan: yellow light would buy strength, not unbind the poison; only the paired neutralizer is a cure.

### 3.3 Rest stop (mechanic + healer + info desk)

Same three transactions: (a) the ship needs a slow **vat-grown** part; (b) the poison has **one antidote, held only by ranking Brigands**; (c) the tracker points to **Bilki**, reachable only by a no-return drop. Divergences are mostly **tone, transaction count, and the length of the clock**:

- **Mechanic clock:** gpt "days" · opus "nine days" · fable "two weeks" · sol-ultra "several days" to local-fold capability and longer to jump, later bypassed by a twelve-minute donor-seed graft.
- **Mechanic comedy:** gpt runs a bit ("I'll restrain it emotionally"); opus plays Vesk as slow-as-soup ("the vat has its own opinions"); fable is deadpan-insult ("you asked it to die and it agreed" / "It's a QUALITY VAT"); sol-ultra treats drives as temperamental organisms — Kara "overfed" hers and the military donor seed snaps at tools.
- **Healer's framing of the squeeze:** opus states it as one thesis line ("Your ship needs longer to heal than your dog has"); gpt/fable imply it; sol-ultra divides it into "Long enough to act. Not long enough to wait," then explicitly revises Kara's yellow-sun assumption.
- **The stasis-tube image:** fable makes it a **pod-window echo** (paw against glass, "glass between them"); opus flattens the decay-curve on the tube; gpt keeps it functional; sol-ultra has Krypto's paw meet Kara's through the glass, then fall just short when she returns before departure.
- **Station identity:** unnamed (gpt) vs Meridian (opus) vs Oasis (fable) vs unnamed but internally mapped (sol-ultra: Berth Twelve, Gate Eight). sol-ultra expands the bureaucracy across a healer, mechanic, Information Clerk and Ticket Agent, and makes medical/repair liens part of the return-shuttle transaction.

## 4. Signature differences, at a glance (objective)

- **Object logic:** fable threads one crossbow through murder → tracker → brawl → climax. sol-ultra uses parallel objects: Father's white pistol carries murder → tracker → recovery → cairn, while the jump-seed drives theft → bar retrieval → escape and is echoed/resolved by the donor-seed repair.
- **Governing rule:** sol-ultra alone plants "a life in your keeping comes before a debt" before the murder, lets Mother use it against Ruthye's departure, lets Ruthye invoke it to save Kara, and resolves it in her return to Tavi. The other drafts concentrate the code language around Ruthye's blood claim, Kara's intervention and Lobo's handover.
- **Dread device:** gpt = numbers; opus = the "hill"; fable = the "tick"; sol-ultra = the contracting viability band plus the ship/patient route lines. Four non-interchangeable presentations of the same clock.
- **Ruthye's early agency:** gpt gives her mechanical competence (valve, turret hinge); opus/fable keep her more observer-until-late; sol-ultra makes her active from the cold-open theft onward (tracking, routing, turret timing/jam, maintenance key, and the Code argument that moves Lobo) while making Kara's repeated body-shielding a source of conflict.
- **Clark:** opus alone stages him in person at the end. gpt/fable call him; sol-ultra keeps him offscreen and ends on Kara and Krypto in his city.
- **Backstory delivery:** gpt states the moral rule outright on the bus; opus leads with imagery ("the quiet after"); fable structures it as sanitized-telling now / full-showing later; sol-ultra gives a detailed slow-collapse account and an explicit prior revenge killing on the bus, then supplies the last family argument in the flashback.
- **Krem's kill:** gun (gpt) vs blade (opus) vs the father's own crossbow (fable) vs the family's white-gripped pistol (sol-ultra).
- **Repair clock length:** days / nine days / two weeks / several days, with sol-ultra alone adding the donor-seed shortcut and using its hull to settle the liens.
- **Home resolution:** sol-ultra separates return, accountability, reunion, grave object, shared meal and birthday flight across four scenes; the other three compress the homecoming and departure into a shorter denouement.
- **Register:** gpt is lean screenplay; opus is image-forward screenplay; fable is annotated treatment; sol-ultra is an expanded, conventionally staged screenplay with the most scene subdivision.
