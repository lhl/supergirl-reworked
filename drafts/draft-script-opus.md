# SUPERGIRL
### *Woman of Tomorrow*

**First-draft screenplay — "opus" draft**

---

*A grief story that has to win an argument about what it means to be good.*

**Tone:** *All-Star Superman* warmth; *For All Seasons* melancholy; *Woman of Tomorrow* lyricism. An off-kilter road trip that happens to cross deep space. The universe is funny; the grief is not. Action is punctuation, never the sentence.

**Principal cast:**

- **KARA ZOR-EL / SUPERGIRL**, 21 — last daughter of a dead world, hiding under a red sun. Numb → engaged → integrated.
- **RUTHYE KNOLL**, 13 — cold, serious, unsettling to adults. Cites her people's Writ, which is armor over grief. Protected → restrained → rescuer → trusted.
- **KREM** — a survivor of a dead world who chose predation and kept choosing it. Not a cartoon. The end of Ruthye's road, made flesh.
- **KRYPTO** — the last of Argo that still breathes.
- **LOBO** — the Main Man. Capricious, not malevolent. What Supergirl looks like without a code.

---

FADE IN:

**EXT. EVELIE — FARM COUNTRY — DUSK (WHICH IS ALWAYS)**

A red sun the color of a cooling coal, low and enormous. It does not seem to be setting. It never seems to be doing anything.

Its light is flat and orange and lands on everything — rows of squat blue-leafed crops, irrigation wheels, a scarecrow stitched from feed sacks — and makes all of it look faintly embalmed.

A farmhouse alone in the fields. Warm yellow lamplight in the windows: the only warm light in the frame.

Smoke from the chimney. No dog barks.

**INT. KNOLL HOMESTEAD — KITCHEN — CONTINUOUS**

The warm light has a source — an oil lamp, a hearth, a table laid for four.

ALDER KNOLL (40s, sunburned, big careful hands) ladles stew. His wife NELL (30s, quick to laugh) passes bread. RUTHYE (13) sits very straight and eats very seriously, as she does everything. PIP (6) kicks the table leg and narrates his food.

**PIP**

*(of a dumpling)* This one is the king. This one is the king's *dog.* The king is going to eat his dog.

**NELL**

Pip.

**PIP**

The dog said it was *fine.*

Alder taps his cup against the table — a small household grace. The others quiet.

**ALDER**

For the light we kept.

**RUTHYE / NELL / PIP**

*(worn smooth by repetition)* For the light we kept.

They eat. Nell catches Alder's eye and smiles.

The door opens. Not kicked — opened, the way you'd open your own door.

KREM stands in it. Late 30s, lean, travel-grimed, a face that was handsome before it decided not to bother. Behind him, three BRIGANDS — armed, unhurried, already looking at the pantry.

No one at the table moves. Pip's fork stops halfway.

Krem takes off his hat. Looks at the stew the way a man looks at a fire he's cold enough to want.

**KREM**

Don't get up.

He pulls the empty fourth chair and sits. Reaches past Alder — without asking — and takes the pie cooling on the sideboard. Sets it in front of himself. Pip has been folded quietly onto Nell's lap.

His men fan out through the house. A cupboard. A chest. Boots on the stairs. Krem doesn't watch them. He watches the family watch him.

**KREM**

*(cutting pie)* We need what a house like this has. Powder, dry stores, fuel cells if you've got them. Rope. Whatever's in the barn worth carrying.

*(a bite)* This is good. Did you make this?

He asks Nell. She doesn't answer. He nods as if she had.

**ALDER**

*(carefully, hands flat on the table)* Take the stores. Take the barn. We won't stop you.

**KREM**

I know.

He eats another bite. Sets the fork down. Settles into something he's said before.

**KREM**

I ate at a table like this once. My mother's. You people love your lamps out here.

*(gesturing with the fork)* My world had a sky you'd have wept to see. And when the end came for it — the end comes for all of them, that's the one law — do you know who came?

Beat. He looks around the warm kitchen as though the answer might be hiding in it.

**KREM**

Nobody. The neighbors we traded with. The good, decent, lawful people at tables exactly like this. They turned up their lamps so they wouldn't have to watch, and they let us go.

*(gently)* I was like you once. So sure. So *haughty.* Before I understood how things are.

**ALDER**

That's not — 

*(choosing each word like ice)* — that's not us. We're farmers. We keep to our own.

It isn't true, and the room knows it. Nell doesn't look at Alder. Ruthye does — a flicker, a child catching a parent in a lie for the first time.

Krem catches all of it. Lets it pass. Goes back to the pie.

**KREM**

Sure.

Upstairs, something breaks. Pip flinches into his mother.

Krem eats. The scrape of his fork fills the room. The Brigands drift back into the doorway, arms full — sacks, rope, a strongbox.

**VORN**

Barn's got fuel cells. Enough.

**KREM**

*(not turning)* Good.

He wipes his mouth. Reaches for his hat. He has what he came for.

And Pip — six years old, holding it in with everything he has — whispers into his mother's collar:

**PIP**

*(barely audible)* I don't like him.

Krem stops. Not angry. Interested. He looks — not at Pip — at Ruthye.

Because Ruthye is looking at him, and has been the whole time, with the flat certainty of a child who is sure the world is fair and that this man is weather that will pass. She isn't afraid of him. She's unimpressed by him.

**KREM**

*(softly, to Ruthye)* You think it isn't true.

Ruthye doesn't answer. That's the answer.

Krem sets his hat back down on the table. Alder is up instantly, putting his body between Krem and the children — and Vorn and a second Brigand have him by the arms before he's taken a full step. Bored. Practiced.

Nell is on her feet with Pip. Ruthye stands.

The room goes loud — Alder straining, Nell shouting, a chair over —

Krem crosses to Nell and strikes her. Open hand. A test, to see who moves.

Alder detonates — throws a man off, gets one step — and is hauled down, a knee in his spine, cheek to his own floor.

The room drops to breathing. Nell on the ground, hand to her face. Pip screaming with no sound. Alder pinned, wrenching, useless.

And it cools. No one is dead. The mother struck but alive, the father pinned but alive, the children untouched. Krem has his fuel cells and his rope. He could pick up his hat and walk out into the red.

He looks down at Alder, glaring up at him with a father's whole hatred.

Then he looks at Ruthye. She has put herself in front of Pip, one arm holding him behind her. Still looking at Krem the way you'd look at a stain. Waiting for him to be over.

Krem holds her eyes. We watch him decide.

**KREM**

*(quietly, almost sad)* I really was like you.

He draws a short blade. Kneels by Alder's pinned head. And — never once looking at the father, eyes on the daughter the whole time — he does it.

We don't see the blade land. We stay on Ruthye's face.

Nell screams. Pip sobs into Ruthye's back — she has pushed his face into her shoulder. She did not look away herself.

Krem stands. Cleans the blade on his own coat. Picks up his hat. At the door he pauses, and says it not like a threat but like a gift:

**KREM**

This is what the world is.

He steps out into the red. His men follow with the family's stores. The door swings on its hinge, not quite shut.

Nell crawls to Alder. Pip is a siren against Ruthye's spine.

Hold on Ruthye. Standing. Her brother shaking against her. Her eyes fixed on the door Krem walked through.

Not grief. Not shock. Rage — already deciding something.

**KREM (V.O.)**

*(a whisper across the cut)* This is what the world is.

**CUT TO:**

**INT. KARA'S SHIP — SLEEPING BERTH — "MORNING" (RED)**

From that whisper — silence. The recycled hum of an old ship.

A cramped berth. Bottles. A star-scout's cockpit gone to seed — laundry over the instruments, a mug growing something, a good jacket (battered, oversized, cracked brown leather) thrown over the pilot's chair like a body.

A woman asleep, not peacefully. KARA ZOR-EL (21, though hard living has aged the edges). She twitches. Her mouth works. Behind her eyes —

**FLASH — ARGO. FRAGMENTS:**

— a sky the wrong color, cracking like glaze —

— a woman's face, ALURA, saying something we can't hear, reaching —

— a small pod, a hatch, a hand on the glass from inside —

— silence —

Kara wakes with a gasp, sitting up. For one second she doesn't know where she is. Then she does.

Her hand is already moving — before her eyes are fully open — to the bottle beside the berth. She finds it. Drinks. Lies back down.

On the pilot's chair, a shape lifts its head: KRYPTO. A big, absurd, snow-white dog, the only clean thing in the ship. He watches her with the patience of an animal who has watched this every morning.

A soft chime. A device on the shelf, blinking.

**SHIP (V.O.)**

*(an old ship's AI, worn to politeness)* Good morning, Kara. You have one saved message —

**KARA**

No.

**SHIP (V.O.)**

It's four days old now.

**KARA**

*(eyes closed)* Ship.

**SHIP (V.O.)**

Playing.

A hologram flickers up — small, blue, glitching. CLARK, kind-faced, recording into a phone somewhere loud and bright. Kara listens lying down, the bottle on her chest.

**CLARK (V.O./HOLO)**

Hey. It's me. Again. You'd think I'd get better at these.

*(then, gentler)* Happy birthday, by the way. I know — you're out there celebrating. Red sun, the whole bit. I get it.

*(a laugh at himself, then softer)* I know roughly where you are. I know what that light does, and why you picked it. I'm not gonna come drag you off some rock — you'd hate me, and you'd be right.

*(the smile goes)* But you don't have to be *nowhere.* There's a whole planet here with your name on the buzzer. Whenever. No expiry.

*(someone off-frame calls him)* — I gotta — okay. Call me back. Or don't. Rao's light, cuz. Come home.

The hologram folds up and dies. Blue gone. Only red left.

On the shelf, a small framed picture, turned face-down.

Krypto pads over, puts his head on the edge of the berth, and whines — quietly.

Now she looks at him. And her face does its one soft thing.

**KARA**

*(hoarse)* Yeah. Okay. Breakfast.

She sits up. Winces. Shrugs into the battered leather jacket — it goes on over a faded blue suit, the color long washed out of it — and takes the bottle with her when she stands.

**INT. KARA'S SHIP — GALLEY / PORTHOLE — CONTINUOUS**

She pours something into a bowl. Krypto inhales it. She doesn't eat. She stands at the porthole with the bottle.

Through the glass: the red sun. The port town below — squat structures, a landing field, flat orange nothing.

She lifts the bottle, looks at the red sun through it, the world gone bloodier through the glass.

**KARA**

*(to no one)* Just a little longer.

She drinks. Krypto looks up from the empty bowl — at her, at the bottle, at the red porthole. His tail doesn't wag.

She scratches his ears. She does not call Clark back.

**CUT TO:**

**EXT. EVELIE — PORT TOWN — "DAY" (RED)**

A frontier port under the coal-red sun. Landing pads, fuel gantries, a customs queue of the strangest travelers you've seen, none of them remarkable to anyone here. A vendor sells something on sticks. Signage in three alphabets, one of them apparently just dents.

Down a side row, a low bar dug half into the ground. A hand-lettered sign:

**THE LONG DUSK** — *always open — the sun sure isn't going anywhere*

**INT. THE LONG DUSK — CONTINUOUS**

Dim, close, red light in slats through the shutters. A dozen patrons. A card game between two things with too many hands. A musician playing an instrument that is mostly gourd. The bartender, GORL — broad, four-eyed, patient — polishes a glass that will never be clean.

In a back booth, in the darkest corner: KARA, the leather jacket on her back, drunk. Not charmingly. An elbow on the table, a glass she's stopped bothering to lift. KRYPTO lies under the booth, alert in a way she isn't. She is not bothering anyone.

The door opens. Red light spills in, and a small silhouette in it.

RUTHYE. A traveling coat much too big for her — a grown man's, cinched twice to fit. A wrapped bundle across her back that is very obviously a sword. Filthy from the road, composed as a judge. She reads the room off a page. Scanning. Hunting.

A RUFFIAN — big, drunk, bored — steps into her path, delighted.

**RUFFIAN**

You lost, sweetpea? This ain't a —

**RUTHYE**

*(not slowing)* Move.

**RUFFIAN**

*(to the room)* She says *move —*

He reaches for her shoulder. What happens is fast and slightly horrible: she catches the wrist, turns it wrong, drops her weight, and puts three hundred pounds of ruffian face-first through a card table, which folds. His knife spins across the floor and stops at her boot. She hasn't changed expression.

The whole bar recalibrates.

Ruthye picks up the knife, sets it on the bar handle-first toward Gorl — *your house, not a thief* — and keeps scanning. Her eyes reach Krypto, travel up to Kara in the booth. She reads Kara head to toe: the jacket, the glass. Files it. Walks over. Sits down across from her with the gravity of a diplomat.

**RUTHYE**

You're not from here.

**KARA**

*(after a moment, amused)* Neither are you.

**RUTHYE**

*(to Gorl, across the room)* Water. Clean, if you have it.

Kara gives Gorl a small nod: *she's with me, I guess.*

**KARA**

Nice throw.

**RUTHYE**

He was in the way.

**KARA**

Where'd a kid learn that?

**RUTHYE**

I'm not a kid. I'm thirteen.

**KARA**

*(ghost of a smile)* Right. My mistake.

Kara pours another. Ruthye watches the pour like an auditor.

**KARA**

What's a thirteen-year-old doing in a port bar on the ugliest planet in the sky?

Ruthye decides how much this drunk is worth. Then recites it — flat, practiced, armor.

**RUTHYE**

Six days ago, men came to my family's house. Their leader took our stores and killed my father. Deliberately. After it was over. As a lesson.

*(straightening)* By the Writ of my people, the debt is mine to collect. The law isn't a feeling. It says the blood is owed and I'm the one it's owed to.

*(to the door, not to Kara)* I tracked them here. They're still on Evelie — I made sure of that. And I need someone who can fight, because I can throw one drunk man but I can't fight ten.

She looks at Kara directly for the first time. It is a job interview.

**RUTHYE**

You look like you used to be able to fight. Before whatever this is.

Kara huffs a laugh and drinks to it.

**KARA**

I'm not muscle for hire, kid.

**RUTHYE**

Ruthye.

**KARA**

Kara. I'm not muscle for hire, Ruthye.

She asks the next thing lightly. She has no idea it's a trapdoor.

**KARA**

You've got people back home still? Somebody who knows where you are?

**RUTHYE**

*(a fact, filed under logistics)* My mother. And my brother. He's six. They're waiting.

Kara goes still. The glass stops halfway down.

She sets it on the table. When she speaks it isn't a lecture. It's low, and it's kind, and it comes out of her before she can stop it.

**KARA**

Ruthye. Go home.

**RUTHYE**

*(the first crack of impatience — she wants a soldier)* The Writ —

**KARA**

I heard you about the Writ.

*(quieter)* Go home. Tonight.

Ruthye holds still. It costs her.

**RUTHYE**

I can't.

**KARA**

The debt'll keep. Your mother won't, forever. Trust me on the order those go in.

**RUTHYE**

You don't understand.

**KARA**

I do. Go home.

Ruthye's jaw sets. And because she is thirteen and cornered and right, by her own lights, she produces evidence.

She reaches into the too-big coat and sets something on the table between them. A grungy metal-and-glass case, about the size of a baseball. Inside it, a crystal turns slowly, glowing a deep steady color. Not local. Stolen.

**RUTHYE**

Their ship's fuel core. I took it out of their raider while they slept off my father's wine. It's why they're still on this planet — they can't leave without it.

*(setting it precisely, a chess piece)* They aren't out there somewhere. They're *here.* Looking for this, and for me. I baited a hook and I'm sitting on it.

A flicker of real alarm cuts through Kara's fog.

**KARA**

Ruthye. Take that, throw it in a river on your way home tonight.

**RUTHYE**

I have one more thing.

She sets a second object down. A battered directional finder, a needle behind cracked glass, dark now.

**RUTHYE**

My father's. Keyed to his good rifle — the one they took. So I can find it wherever they carry it. It's faint. It only points when I wake it up.

Her hand goes to the switch.

**KARA**

*(a beat too slow)* Don't —

**RUTHYE**

I want you to see it's real.

She turns it on.

The needle swings. The glass floods with light — not a distant hunting glow. Bright, and brightening. And the needle isn't pointing out the door toward the horizon. It swings toward the door itself.

Kara reads it a full second before Ruthye does.

**KARA**

*(low)* Rao.

*(realizing)* The crystal. They can track the *crystal —*

The door of the Long Dusk slams open. Red light, and men in it.

KREM — and behind him, the Brigands. Six, seven. Vorn among them.

For one frozen instant, Ruthye and Krem see each other. Recognition — hers total, his a beat slower, then landing.

**KREM**

*(to Ruthye, delighted, terrible)* You came all this way.

The bar erupts.

**THE BRAWL — fast and ugly, not a superhero fight:**

Ruthye lunges for the crystal — Kara sweeps it off the table into her own jacket and shoves Ruthye behind her with the other arm.

Kara comes out of the booth. She catches the first Brigand's club and it stops like it hit a wall; she throws him and he goes *too far,* through a shutter, red light blasting in. But she's slow to recover. She stumbles on the follow. A very tough, very drunk woman fighting seven ready men in a small room.

Krypto is magnificent and idiotic — a white blur, latching onto arms, dragging men down, taking a chair across the back and not caring.

Krem hangs back by the door, eyes on Kara's jacket.

A Brigand at the broken shutter raises a squat crossbow, its bolt tipped with something that beads wet and dark. He sights on Kara's back as she's tangled with two others.

**RUTHYE**

*(seeing it)* Behind — !

The bolt fires. Kara turns, half a beat too slow —

— and Krypto is already in the air.

He takes it. The bolt buries in the white of his shoulder. He yelps — a sound that goes through the whole bar — and drops, twisting, snapping at the shaft.

The world stops for exactly one person.

**KARA**

*No —*

Something that isn't drunk and isn't tired comes up out of her. She grabs the nearest thing — a heavy oak table — and *throws* it. Not at a man. Through the *wall.* The whole side of the Long Dusk blows out into the red street. It empties her. She staggers, catches herself, gasping.

And when Krypto yelps again on the floor, her whole world collapses down to the dog. She drops to him, hands on the wound, everything else gone.

That's when Krem moves. Unhurried, stepping over a groaning Brigand. He crouches beside the gasping woman, close, and reaches into her torn jacket and takes the crystal — plucks his ship's heart back out from under the one person who could have stopped him.

Kara feels the hand. Half-turns. Reaches — and has nothing. Her fingers close on air.

**KREM**

*(pocketing it, almost gentle)* There it is. That's all I came for.

*(to Ruthye, across the fallen dog)* Told you.

He stands. To his crew:

**KREM**

Out. We're done here.

They go — fast, practiced — out through the new hole in the wall. Krem last, the crystal in his coat.

Gone.

Krypto on the floor with a bolt in him. Kara bent over him, too weak to have stopped any of it.

**INT. THE LONG DUSK — WRECKAGE — MOMENTS LATER**

Dust settling. Red light pours through the hole where a wall was. Gorl stands slowly, all four eyes on the ruin.

Kara has Krypto's head in her lap. The bolt is still in him. He pants wrong. Around the wound, the white fur is going a color fur shouldn't.

**KARA**

Okay. I've got you. Don't do that — keep breathing, that's an order —

She reaches for the bolt. Ruthye's hand catches her wrist — suddenly calm and fast.

**RUTHYE**

Don't pull it. If it's barbed you'll tear him worse, and it's poisoned — see the bead? Pull the shaft, you leave the poison and open the vein. Cut around it, or get him somewhere that can.

Kara stares at her.

**KARA**

*(a broken laugh, near tears)* Who *are* you.

**RUTHYE**

Someone whose father was a hunter. Where's the nearest bay?

Kara looks at her own hands, which minutes ago threw a table through a wall and could not throw it half a second sooner.

**KARA**

My ship. It's close. Junk, but it's got a medbay.

She gets an arm under the dog. He's enormous; she's depleted; it's a struggle that should be nothing and isn't. Ruthye takes his hindquarters without being asked. Between them they carry him out through the hole in the wall, into the red.

Gorl watches them go. Looks at the knife left handle-first on his bar. Sighs.

**CUT TO:**

**INT. KARA'S SHIP — MEDBAY — CONTINUOUS**

Cramped. Half the panels dark. But it's a medbay. Krypto on the slab, a scanning ring dropping over him, running amber and red. Kara works fast — her hands are sure even when the rest of her isn't. Ruthye stands back, watching an adult work.

**SHIP (V.O.)**

Foreign compound in the bloodstream. It's binding to his cells faster than his body can clear it. I can slow it. I can't stop it.

**KARA**

Slow it how much.

**SHIP (V.O.)**

*(a pause)* Enough to give him time. Not a lot of it. He's a very strong animal, Kara. That's the only reason there's a number at all.

A readout resolves on the wall — not a clean countdown but a decay curve, a line sloping down toward a shaded band. Ruthye reads it without being told how.

**RUTHYE**

It's not a clock. It's a hill.

**KARA**

*(grim)* And we're on the wrong side of it.

She braces both hands over the sleeping dog. Explains it as a plan forming in a panic.

**KARA**

Where I'm from — my kind, and him — a red sun makes us weak. Empties us out. It's why I'm like this. But a *yellow* sun fills us back up. Not medicine exactly. More like — he's a plant that's been in a closet. Put us under the right light and we come *back.* If I get him under a yellow sun, he might burn the poison out on his own.

**RUTHYE**

Where's the nearest yellow sun.

Kara's already at the cockpit door.

**KARA**

Too far. But maybe not too far if I'm stupid about it.

**INT. KARA'S SHIP — COCKPIT — CONTINUOUS**

Kara drops into the pilot's seat, sweeps laundry off the console. Ruthye buckles in behind, sword across her knees. Krypto visible through the medbay hatch, the curve glowing over him.

One console light has clearly been blinking a long time, unheeded.

**SHIP (V.O.)**

Before we do anything ambitious, I should note — as I have on eleven previous occasions — that the drive-seed is past service life and the reserve is —

**KARA**

I know.

**SHIP (V.O.)**

— low. It's very low, Kara.

**KARA**

*(strapping in)* Plot me the closest yellow star. Then redline the jump.

**SHIP (V.O.)**

*(a beat)* The nearest suitable star is beyond our safe range on a healthy seed. On this one —

**KARA**

Redline it.

**SHIP (V.O.)**

*(doing as told)* For the record, I raised concerns.

**KARA**

Noted. Loudly. Go.

She points the ship's nose away from the red sun and lights the drive.

**EXT. SPACE — CONTINUOUS**

The little ship claws off Evelie and jumps — a rough, ugly leap, like a stone skipped by a tired arm.

**INT. KARA'S SHIP — COCKPIT — CONTINUOUS**

Everything shakes. Warnings stack faster than the ship can voice them. Kara flies it like she's holding a wound closed with her hands. Behind her, Ruthye is white-knuckled but silent, reading Kara, not the instruments.

**SHIP (V.O.)**

Drive-seed temperature past —

**KARA**

Come on. One good jump, old girl —

**SHIP (V.O.)**

Kara. We are not going to make the star.

**KARA**

*Don't —*

Something under the deck lets go — a deep, wrong crunch. The straining engine-glow guts out. The ship yaws; the little yellow star ahead slides out of the canopy.

Kara fights it. There's nothing to fight it with. She sags.

**SHIP (V.O.)**

*(gently)* The drive-seed's gone. There's a service waypoint within reach on thrusters. It's the only thing within reach on thrusters.

Kara stares at the star they didn't get to. Behind them the decay curve keeps sloping. She presses the heels of her hands into her eyes.

**KARA**

*(hollow)* Course to the waypoint.

**SHIP (V.O.)**

Setting course. It's called Meridian.

*(trying to help)* They'll have a doctor.

Kara turns and looks back at Krypto. Ruthye is looking at her.

**RUTHYE**

*(an observation, not unkind)* You did everything right. It just wasn't enough.

**KARA**

*(a breath of a laugh, no humor)* Yeah. Story of my life.

The dead ship swings, limping on thrusters toward a distant smudge of light.

**DISSOLVE TO:**

**EXT. MERIDIAN WAYSTATION — ESTABLISHING**

And the color detonates.

MERIDIAN hangs in the void like a truck stop that ate a carnival — a fluorescent snarl of docking arms, neon vendor signage in forty languages, a rotating ring that's either a hotel or a hospital or both. Ships of impossible shapes queue at pumps. A tow-drone hauls a dead freighter past a cheerful sign:

**WELCOME TO MERIDIAN — "THE HALFWAY EVERYWHERE" — HAVE YOUR CHITS READY**

No sun here. No red, no yellow. Just commerce, buzzing, in the dark.

Kara's sad little ship limps into a cradle among giants.

**INT. MERIDIAN — REPAIR APRON — CONTINUOUS**

Grease, steam, the shriek of tools. A MECHANIC — VESK, a large, unhurried, many-jointed being who speaks slowly because everything about them is slow — runs a scanner along Kara's opened drive housing. Kara hovers. Ruthye takes in the enormity of the place with eyes she's trying to keep narrow.

**VESK**

*(terrible news delivered like weather)* Mm. Seed's cooked. All the way. See how it's gone glassy? That's cooked.

**KARA**

So replace it. How fast?

**VESK**

Oh, we don't *stock* 'em. Nobody does. You don't keep a drive-seed, they go off.

**KARA**

Then where —

**VESK**

We *grow* it.

*(the good news)* Got a vat right there. Beautiful vat. Put a starter in, keep it warm, keep it fed, and in — 

*(a being for whom time is soup)* — oh. Nine days? Eleven if she's fussy.

Kara closes her eyes.

**KARA**

I don't have nine days.

**VESK**

*(immovable)* Vat doesn't care what you have. Vat has its own opinions.

*(patting the housing)* I'll start one. Clinic's up the ring. You'll want the clinic.

**CUT TO:**

**INT. MERIDIAN — DOCK CLINIC — CONTINUOUS**

Cleaner, barely. A row of vertical stabilizing tubes along one wall — great cylinders of faintly glowing amber fluid, a couple occupied by shapes we don't look at. A DOCK-HEALER — DR. SILT, brisk, six-fingered, a bedside manner sanded smooth by ten thousand doomed travelers — reads Krypto's chart off a floating slate. Krypto, sedated, breathes in little assisted hitches.

**DR. SILT**

Your ship's diagnosis is right as far as it goes. Poison. But it couldn't name it. I can. This is Brigand work — the bolt-tip compound. I pull it out of raid survivors twice a month.

**KARA**

Then you can treat it. You have the —

You must live.

Kara, wrecked, nods. Barely.

Zor-El — hands on her shoulders, the pod hatch open behind her.

**ZOR-EL**

Promise me you'll be good.

Beat. Young Kara looks at her father. At her mother. At the ending sky.

**YOUNG KARA**

*(a whisper)* I promise.

The pod. The hatch. A hand on the glass from outside, a hand on the glass from inside, matched for a second through the barrier. The launch. The silence after.

**END FLASHBACK.**

**EXT. BILKI — UPPER ATMOSPHERE — LOBO'S BIKE — CONTINUOUS**

The blue-flamed bike claws up through the poisoned sky. Lobo hunched over the bars, Ruthye clinging on inside her wobbling rainbow bubble, one arm locked around Kara — Kara slumped against the machine, the shredded jacket fallen open around her shoulders like a shawl.

The nuclear winter thins as they rise. The gray gets brighter, then thinner —

Kara, eyes closed, a single tear breaking loose and floating free.

Silence. The bike. The last of the gray tearing away in rags.

They punch through the top of the dead sky into —

**BLINDING. YELLOW. LIGHT.**

The sun floods everything. It pours over the bike, the bubble, the woman slumped against the machine.

Lobo cuts the climb. Kara's limp body drifts up off the bike's flank — floating now in raw sunlight, unshielded, curled fetal, small. Not cruciform.

For a moment, nothing.

Then her body takes a breath. Reflexive, involuntary, whole-body — the gasp of something being born. Her chest fills. Her spine straightens.

The jacket — too shredded now to hold together — slips off her shoulders and drifts away behind her into the black, tumbling, gone.

And under it, what she's worn the whole time: the suit. Blue and red, the House of El crest at her chest — bloodied, washed out, gray at the seams. No cape.

Then the yellow light finds it. The wounds close — sword-cut, shrapnel, turret-tears knitting shut. And the color floods back into the suit the way it floods back into her: the drained gray-blue going vivid, the red going deep, the crest bright. Both of them emptied by the red sun. Both of them filled by the yellow.

Kara's eyes open. Clear. No fog.

She turns in the gold and finds Ruthye — inside her silly bubble, crying, streaked with ash and Kara's blood.

Kara reaches out. Pulls the bubble, and the girl in it, in against her. Holds her. Under the sun.

Off to the side, straddling his idling bike in the full glory of it, **LOBO** watches two people hug in the sunlight, and is *mortified.*

He kicks the bike into gear. Guns it, already accelerating away from the sunlight and the crying. Over his shoulder, as the blue flame hauls him off into the black:

**LOBO**

Fuckin' *codes,* man.

Gone. A blue streak, dwindling.

And then it's just the two of them, floating in the yellow light above a dead world. Kara, whole. Ruthye, held. The sun on both of them.

**FADE TO WHITE.**

**EXT. BILKI — UPPER ATMOSPHERE — CONTINUOUS**

From white — into blue-gold clarity. Kara, floating, whole, the light on her. She looks down at the gray lid of the dead world and her jaw sets. She gathers Ruthye — bubble and all — in close and safe, and drops.

**EXT. BILKI — DESCENT — CONTINUOUS**

She punches through the gray cloud-deck and a SONIC BOOM rolls out ahead of her, blowing a clean hole in the murk, a shaft of yellow light stabbing all the way to the ash for the first time in years.

**EXT. BILKI — BRIGAND DEPOT — CONTINUOUS**

She sets down light as a leaf, Ruthye beside her, the bubble dissolving with a last apologetic chime.

Lobo's gallery — bodies, wreckage, the crushed hauler, the fires. Kara scans the field in a glance. A row of Brigand shuttles sits half-buried along the depot wall. One is warmed up: running lights on, ramp down, engines ticking. The ride Krem is trying to use to run.

And near it — Krem.

He's gotten some of himself back together. Functional. Desperate. A huge duffel of Brigand plunder — gold, gems — in one fist. With the other he's wrenching a heavy energy weapon off the corpse of one of his crew. He fits it to his left arm. It attaches with a grisly, wet *thunk,* biting in. He grimaces.

Kara starts forward. Ruthye moves with her, hand going to the sword she's picked up off the ash.

**KARA**

*(without turning, quiet, absolute)* Stay back.

**RUTHYE**

I have the Writ, I —

Kara turns and looks at her. Not angry. Not pleading. Just a look. Ruthye stops. Stays back.

Krem straightens, the gun-arm humming. He sees Supergirl — whole now, radiant, terrible — and can't hold her eyes. His own are still red-rimmed. He glances once at Ruthye, past Kara. Looks away from her too.

He gestures, small and tired, at the whole gray world.

**KREM**

*(quietly)* Look around you.

*(a breath)* There was never anyone to save us.

He raises the poison bow — an arrow already nocked, tip beading wet and dark. Kara's eyes go to one thing only: the small vial on a chain at Krem's neck.

**KREM**

*(flat, aiming)* You should have stayed on your rock.

He looses the arrow.

**THE NON-FIGHT.**

The arrow leaves the bow — and it's *slow.* Absurdly slow. It hangs in the air, barely crawling.

Because Kara is already off the ground. A shockwave blooms out from where she stood as she crosses the courtyard faster than sound. Krem's eyes have only begun to widen.

She plucks the poison arrow out of the air between two fingers, without slowing.

She reaches him. She brings her hand down on the poison bow and knocks it out of his grip — rougher, harder, than she needed to.

**SNAP BACK TO REALTIME —**

— and Krem howls. The shockwave has blasted him meters back across the ash. He lands hard. His right arm bends a way arms don't. The bow is gone, shattered.

Kara stands over the wreck of him, the arrow still pinched in her fingers — and looks at her own hand. At the too-hard hit. At the man screaming with a broken arm she didn't need to break.

She presses two fingers to her own temple. Steadies. Makes herself gentle.

**KARA**

*(reaching for calm)* Listen. I'm not here for you. I just want the antidote. Around your neck. That's all — I need it to save —

A huge RED GLOW floods the frame.

Krem — on his back, broken-armed, done — has overloaded the energy weapon on his other arm. Sparking, swelling, venting light. Not aimed. A kamikaze. He isn't even looking at her. He's looking at the gray sky.

The screen goes red — then, instantly, WHITE.

**EXT. BILKI — WIDE — CONTINUOUS**

From a distance: a single concussive flash on the dead ground, and a red-white arc of energy tearing across the gray sky, over the horizon, gone.

Where Supergirl was standing, there is nothing but smoke and settling ash.

Silence after the blast. The old fires burn.

And through the smoke — a figure. Small. Walking, not running. Toward the wreck of Krem.

**RUTHYE.**

Sword drawn. Face set. Nothing in her says mercy. Nothing says hesitation.

She reaches Krem. He's a ruin — bleeding, burnt, one arm broken, the other a charred smoking stump. But the little vial still hangs at his throat, untouched.

Krem looks up at her. Not the evangelist from the kitchen. Not the philosopher who killed her father. Tired. Hollowed out.

**KREM**

*(a rasp, nothing left in it)* ...Just do it.

Ruthye looks at him. The monster from her kitchen isn't here. Just a tired, broken man in the rubble of the world that made him.

She raises the sword.

She *slashes* — and the blade takes the *chain.* Not the neck.

The vial drops — and Ruthye catches it, lifted on the flat of her father's sword. She turns away from Krem. She does not look back.

Behind her —

**KARA.**

Standing in the settling smoke, whole, unmarked. She's been there the whole time. She did not intervene. She could have.

She didn't.

The look on her face as Ruthye turns around holding the antidote instead of blood: relief, mostly. And under it, brighter — pride.

Ruthye reaches her. Holds out the vial. Their hands meet around it. Kara closes her fingers over Ruthye's and the cure both. Then pulls her in. They hold each other, both crying now, in a shaft of yellow light.

**RUTHYE**

*(into Kara's shoulder)* Let's go home.

They walk to the warmed-up shuttle, ramp down. They climb aboard. The ramp closes.

Behind them, in the smoke, the wreck of a man who may be alive or may be dead.

The camera does not go to him. It stays with Ruthye, walking away, not looking back.

The shuttle lifts off the dead world, punches up through the gray, and is gone.

**CUT TO:**

**INT. MERIDIAN — DOCK CLINIC — LATER**

The amber tube. Krypto floating, the decay curve on its side sloped almost into the shaded band.

The tube drains. The seal breaks. Silt and Kara ease the big white dog out onto the gurney. Limp. Cold. Barely there.

No fanfare. Kara hands Silt the vial. Silt loads it, finds the vein, pushes it home.

The needle. Then — the breath.

Krypto's chest fills. His eyes crack open. His head lifts off the gurney, ears swiveling, finding the one smell he was looking for.

Kara puts her forehead down against his. His tail thumps the gurney, weak, then less weak.

**KARA**

*(a wet laugh into his fur)* Hey. Hey, dummy. There you are.

Across the clinic, Ruthye watches, the ghost of a real smile on her. Through the bay windows, Vesk gives a slow thumbs-up: Kara's little ship sits repaired on the apron, a salvaged Brigand drive crystal seated where the cooked seed used to be.

**DISSOLVE TO:**

**EXT. EVELIE — KNOLL HOMESTEAD — "EVENING" (RED, WARM)**

The red sun again — the same cooling coal on the same horizon. But graded warm now, golden-hour instead of embalmed.

The blue-leafed crops. The irrigation wheels. The farmhouse, warm light in the windows.

**EXTREME CLOSE-UP:** a streak of white, launching —

KRYPTO hits Kara full in the chest, ninety pounds of resurrected dog, knocking her flat into the field-dirt, licking her face, tail a blur, alive, warm, whole.

Kara laughs — really laughs — and lets him.

**PULL BACK —**

— and it's not just Kara. Ruthye stands beside them. Behind Ruthye: PIP, gawking at the enormous white dog and the flying woman; and NELL, one hand on Pip's shoulder, the other pressed to her mouth, looking at her daughter like she's looking at a sunrise.

Kara's repaired ship waits in the background, gleaming faintly in the red-gold.

Kara gets up, brushing off dirt, Krypto orbiting her. She and Ruthye stand a little apart from the family.

Ruthye looks at Kara. At the ship. Wistful.

**RUTHYE**

*(quiet)* I wish I could go with you. But I have —

She doesn't finish. She nods back over her shoulder at Nell and Pip.

Kara looks at the family. At the warm windows.

**KARA**

I would trade the world for what you have.

*(a beat)* This is where you belong.

They stand in it a moment. The red-gold light. The dog. The family in the door.

Then the solemnity cracks, and underneath it is an impish, delighted grin.

**KARA**

*(brightening)* ...but, you know. We never *did* get a chance to finish celebrating my birthday.

Ruthye looks at her.

And the serious face — the code-citing, ruffian-disarming, Writ-reciting face — cracks open.

She *giggles.* The weight comes off her shoulders all at once.

**KARA**

*(already moving toward the ship, Krypto bounding ahead)* Come on. Three systems over, the pies are the size of a *table,* you have not lived —

**RUTHYE**

*(chasing, laughing)* We can't just — my mother —

**KARA**

*(calling to the doorway, waving)* We'll have her back tonight! Tomorrow at the latest!

Nell laughs and shakes her head and waves them off. Pip is already running after the dog.

Kara and Ruthye and Krypto pile into the little repaired ship. It lights up — healthy, orange-gold — and lifts off the field, banks, and climbs.

**FINAL IMAGE:** the little ship crossing a fiery red sky — the same red sun that opened the film, just the ordinary blaze of a living evening now. And inside it, Ruthye, laughing. Free.

**FADE OUT.**

**MID-CREDITS**

**INT. METROPOLIS — HIGH APARTMENT — NIGHT**

A window. Beyond it, a skyline — gleaming, alive, a whole city of lit windows. Clean yellow-white light.

Kara stands at the glass, looking out. No jacket now — a blue collar just showing at the open neck of an ordinary shirt. A mug of something warm in her hand. No bottle in frame.

Behind her, softer, out of focus: CLARK, in the kitchen doorway, sleeves rolled, at home in his own home. He isn't making a thing of it. He just watches her a moment.

**CLARK**

*(gentle, from across the room)* ...Kara. I'm just glad you're back safe.

Kara doesn't turn from the window. But something eases.

Her face in the reflection: sad. But present. Not numb. Not fogged.

**KARA**

*(to the glass, quiet)* Maybe I'll stick around a bit longer this time.

A breath. The city lights.

**KARA**

I mean. It's good to be —

*(and the word comes out simple and true)* — home.

Behind her, a streak of white: KRYPTO, gleefully destroying something he absolutely should not be — a couch cushion, a houseplant, the leg of an expensive table. Alive. A terror.

Kara turns from the window, catches him mid-crime.

**KARA**

*(no heat in it at all)* Hey — *hey* — that's the third one, buddy —

Clark, wisely, keeps his distance from the dog. Kara goes to save the houseplant. Home. The city glows behind her.

**FADE OUT.**
