## FADE IN:

### EXT. EVELIE — RUTHYE'S HOMESTEAD — RED EVENING

A red sun hangs low over a farm world that looks almost ordinary until the light touches it wrong.

Wind moves through pale grain. A small house glows warm against the dying sky.

Inside: laughter.

### INT. HOMESTEAD KITCHEN — CONTINUOUS

A family meal. Simple food. A pie cooling at the center of the table.

RUTHYE, thirteen, watches everything. Too serious for her age, but not joyless. Her younger BROTHER eats with both hands. MOTHER corrects him. FATHER pretends not to laugh.

BROTHER  
I washed.

MOTHER  
You showed your hands to the water. That is not the same thing.

FATHER  
In his defense, the water looked impressed.

Ruthye almost smiles.

A knock.

Not a crash. Not a raid. A polite knock.

The family stills.

Father rises. Opens the door.

KREM stands outside with three BRIGANDS behind him. Dusty. Armed. Tired from travel. Not theatrical.

KREM  
Evening.

Father looks at the weapons. Then at his children.

FATHER  
Can I help you?

Krem steps inside as though invited.

KREM  
You can try.

The Brigands enter. They fan out through the house. One checks shelves. One eyes the back rooms. One stays near the door.

Krem sits at the table. Takes a fork. Helps himself to pie.

KREM  
Smells like somebody remembers how to live.

Mother puts a hand on Brother's shoulder.

FATHER  
Whatever you need, take it. No one here wants trouble.

Krem chews. Considers.

KREM  
Weapons. Fuel stores. Route maps if you keep them. Anything that cuts, burns, opens, seals, or flies.

FATHER  
We are farmers.

KREM  
Everyone is something until they are hungry.

Ruthye stares at him.

Krem notices.

KREM  
That look.

No one answers.

KREM  
I had that look once.

He eats another bite.

KREM  
My world had a thousand laws. We had treaties. Flags. Oaths. We had songs about mercy so old nobody remembered who wrote them.

The Brigands keep searching.

KREM  
Then the sky changed. Ships came. Not all at once. That would have been honest. First came the merchants. Then the advisers. Then men with soft hands who said, truly, there was nothing they could do.

Father's face tightens.

KREM  
Your people traded with them.

FATHER  
No.

Krem looks to him. Mildly interested.

FATHER  
No. Whatever happened to you, we had no part in it.

KREM  
You sure?

FATHER  
Yes.

KREM  
That's good.

Beat.

KREM  
Certainty is such a comfort to children.

Ruthye's jaw sets.

Krem smiles faintly at her.

KREM  
I was like you once. Haughty. Certain the world had a shape, and if someone broke it, the world itself would object.

MOTHER  
Please.

KREM  
Before my world was destroyed. Before I saw how things really are.

One Brigand returns with a bundle of tools and an old rifle.

BRIGAND  
Found this.

FATHER  
That is my father's.

KREM  
Then he won't miss it.

Father steps forward before he can stop himself.

FATHER  
Leave that.

The room changes.

Krem looks at Father. Then, with no heat, backhands Mother.

Brother screams. Ruthye rises so fast her chair falls.

Father lunges. The Brigands seize him.

For a moment everything is noise: Mother's gasp, Brother crying, Ruthye breathing through her teeth.

Then it cools.

The Brigands have Father restrained. Mother is alive. Brother is tucked against Ruthye's side. Krem has the weapons. The supplies. Everything he came for.

The off-ramp sits there in the room.

Krem stands.

KREM  
There. See? We can all survive an ugly evening.

He walks toward the door.

Stops.

Looks back at Ruthye.

She is shielding her brother with one arm. Her other hand is wrapped around a small kitchen knife.

Not shaking.

Krem sees the defiance. The belief that this is an aberration. That someday someone will set the world right.

He turns back.

FATHER  
No.

KREM  
No?

FATHER  
Take what you came for.

KREM  
I am.

Krem draws his weapon.

Mother knows before the children do.

MOTHER  
Please, no.

Krem keeps his eyes on Ruthye.

KREM  
This is what the world is.

He kills Father.

Not in the scuffle. After it. Deliberate. A lesson.

Ruthye's brother buries his face against her. Ruthye holds him so he does not see.

Krem lowers the weapon.

KREM  
Now you know.

He leaves with his men.

Silence.

Mother crawls to Father.

Ruthye does not move. Her brother shakes against her. Her arms hold life. Her eyes stay fixed on the door.

Not grief.

Rage.

## CUT TO BLACK.

### INT. KARA'S SHIP — SLEEPING COMPARTMENT — RED MORNING

Silence.

Fragments of a nightmare: a city under a cracking sky. White towers bending. People running. A mother's hand on glass. A father's voice swallowed by alarm.

KARA ZOR-EL wakes gasping.

Her hand finds the bottle before her eyes fully open.

She drinks.

The compartment is dim and messy. Clothes. Tools. Empty containers. A battered oversized leather jacket lies half on the floor, half on the bed.

A red sun glares through the porthole.

A device blinks: MISSED MESSAGE — CLARK.

Kara stares at it. Plays it.

CLARK (V.O.)  
Hey. It's me. Again. You don't have to call back with anything profound. Just call back so I know you're somewhere with breathable air. If you need space, take space. If you need a ride, say the word. If you need me to shut up, call and tell me to shut up.

A warm little pause.

CLARK (V.O.)  
Happy birthday, Kara. Come home when you can.

Message ends.

Kara sits with that.

She does not call back.

She drinks again.

KRYPTO, a big white dog with an impossible alertness, lifts his head from the floor. He whines.

KARA  
Don't look at me like that.

Krypto keeps looking.

KARA  
I'm up.

She is not.

Krypto puts his chin on the bed.

KARA  
Fine. I'm considering up.

She grabs the leather jacket and pulls it around herself like armor.

---

### EXT. EVELIE PORT — SERVICE YARDS — DAY

Red daylight over a working port: cargo rigs, fuel crawlers, alien livestock in transit pens, ships in various states of legality.

Ruthye moves through it like a shadow that has studied maps.

She is not wandering. She knows exactly which ship she is looking for.

Ahead, the Brigand ship sits in a restricted berth. Its hull is scarred with old wars and bad repairs. Two Brigands argue with a PORT OFFICIAL beside a fuel line.

PORT OFFICIAL  
Your departure clearance is suspended until the customs hold clears.

BRIGAND  
Customs hold?

PORT OFFICIAL  
Weapons declaration irregularity, biological contamination flag, and one unpaid berth invoice from six cycles ago.

BRIGAND  
We weren't here six cycles ago.

PORT OFFICIAL  
Then disputing it will be simple. Window thirty-two.

The Brigand looks toward window thirty-two: a line of furious travelers that may be older than the port.

Ruthye slips beneath the fuel gantry.

She holds the tracker keyed to her father's weapon. Its light pulses faintly toward the ship's cargo bay.

A Brigand inside laughs. The sound freezes her for half a breath.

She keeps moving.

Under the ship, she finds the maintenance cradle: a grungy metal-and-glass case nested in the exposed underbelly. A glowing drive crystal turns slowly inside it.

Ruthye pulls a small tool from her sleeve. Not professional. Farm-made. Sharp enough.

She works the latch.

Inside the ship, Krem's voice carries.

KREM (O.S.)  
If the port wants a form, give them a body shaped like a form.

A Brigand laughs.

Ruthye's hand slips. The tool cuts her palm.

She does not make a sound.

The latch opens.

The crystal case drops into her arms, heavier than expected. The ship's engines cough and die down to a sick idle.

Inside: alarm tones.

BRIGAND (O.S.)  
Core fault!

Ruthye runs.

A Brigand leans from the cargo bay and sees her.

BRIGAND  
Hey!

Ruthye ducks under the fuel line as the Brigand fires. The shot bursts a vapor pipe behind her. Steam fills the berth.

She vanishes into it with the drive core clutched to her chest.

### EXT. EVELIE PORT — ALLEY — MOMENTS LATER

Ruthye presses herself into a narrow alley between cargo stacks, breathing hard.

She opens her bleeding palm. Looks at it. Closes it again around the handle of her wrapped sword.

The drive core glows in its case.

Her tracker still points toward the port.

Ruthye looks toward the bar district.

Not helpless. Not safe. Moving.

---

### INT. EVELIE PORT BAR — DAY

A rough port bar under dead red light. The clientele is hard, loud, tired, and armed.

Kara sits alone in a booth, drinking. Krypto sleeps under the table with one eye open.

A RUFFIAN blocks the entrance, laughing too loudly at his own importance.

RUTHYE enters.

She carries a wrapped sword and a small pack. She looks thirteen and not thirteen at all.

RUFFIAN
Children's door is somewhere else.

RUTHYE  
Move.

RUFFIAN
Ask nice.

Ruthye takes his wrist, steps inside his balance, and puts him face-first into a table. Clean. Efficient. The room goes quiet.

Ruthye takes his knife and sets it on the bar.

RUTHYE  
He dropped this.

Kara watches despite herself.

Ruthye scans the room. Her gaze lands on Kara. The jacket. The posture. The dog. The fact that everyone gives her space.

Ruthye approaches.

RUTHYE  
Are you the strongest person here?

KARA  
Depends what time it is.

RUTHYE  
I need someone strong.

KARA  
That's a terrible way to start a conversation.

RUTHYE  
A man named Krem killed my father. I have tracked him to this port. My law demands I hunt him and take vengeance.

Kara blinks. Too much truth too fast.

KARA  
Your law.

RUTHYE  
Yes.

KARA  
How old are you?

RUTHYE  
Old enough to know my law.

KARA  
That's not the same thing.

Ruthye sits opposite her without asking.

KARA  
Sure. Join me.

RUTHYE  
You are drunk.

KARA  
Observant.

RUTHYE  
Can you still fight?

Kara glances down at Krypto.

KARA  
Not as well as my dog.

RUTHYE  
Then I will hire the dog.

Krypto's tail thumps once.

Kara almost laughs, then doesn't.

KARA  
You have family?

Ruthye's face closes.

RUTHYE  
A mother. A brother.

Kara goes still.

RUTHYE  
They wait at home.

The word home hits Kara harder than any punch.

KARA  
Go back to them.

RUTHYE  
No.

KARA  
Go home.

RUTHYE  
My father cannot go home.

KARA  
Your mother can still hold you. Your brother can still see your face. That is not nothing.

RUTHYE  
I did not say it was nothing.

KARA  
You're treating it like something you can come back to after you're done becoming someone else.

Ruthye leans forward.

RUTHYE  
You do not know me.

KARA  
I know this road.

RUTHYE  
Then you know where it leads.

Kara looks at the bottle.

KARA  
Yes.

RUTHYE  
Good. Then you can guide me.

KARA  
No.

Ruthye reaches into her pack and sets a grungy metal-and-glass case on the table. Inside: a glowing drive crystal.

RUTHYE  
They are still here because of this.

Kara sobers a fraction.

KARA  
What is that?

RUTHYE  
Their ship's fuel core.

KARA  
You stole a Brigand drive core.

RUTHYE  
Yes.

KARA  
From the people who killed your father.

RUTHYE  
Yes.

KARA  
That is... profoundly stupid.

RUTHYE  
It worked.

She sets a second device on the table: a tracker.

RUTHYE  
This is keyed to one of my father's weapons. He took it. It points faintly until aligned.

KARA  
Don't turn that on.

RUTHYE  
Why?

KARA  
Because if you can track him, he can probably track that.

Ruthye turns it on.

The device glows intensely bright.

Kara stares.

KARA  
Of course.

The bar door slams open.

Krem enters with Brigands.

KREM  
There you are.

Ruthye rises.

Krem sees her. Recognition. Amusement.

KREM  
Little knife at the table.

RUTHYE  
Murderer.

Krem looks to Kara.

KREM  
And you brought a drunk.

KARA  
She found me like this.

The room explodes.

Brigands move for the drive crystal. Ruthye draws her sword. Kara comes up too fast for someone that drunk — a residual blur — and hammers a Brigand through a chair.

Then she stumbles.

A second Brigand clocks her across the jaw. She feels it. She should not feel it that much.

Krypto launches from under the table, jaws clamping on an armored arm.

KARA  
Krypto!

A Brigand raises a bolt weapon at Kara.

Krypto leaps.

The poison bolt hits him.

Krypto yelps and crashes into the floor.

For Kara, the room vanishes.

KARA  
No.

Krem snatches the drive crystal.

KREM  
We're done.

Ruthye cuts at him. He parries, not impressed.

KREM  
Still looking at the world like it owes you shape.

Kara roars and throws a heavy table through the wall. Red-sun residue, grief, and panic in one burst.

The Brigands retreat through the wreckage.

Kara drops beside Krypto. He pants too fast. Black veins spider from the wound.

Ruthye watches Krem escape.

KARA  
Help me lift him.

RUTHYE  
Krem—

KARA  
Help me lift my dog.

Ruthye obeys.

---

### EXT. EVELIE PORT BAR — CONTINUOUS

Krem and the Brigands spill into the red daylight.

A battered BRIGAND SHIP waits at the edge of the port, engines already coughing hot. Not stolen. Not improvised. Their own way out, pinned here only because Ruthye had taken what it needed to leave.

A Brigand clutches his bleeding arm where Krypto bit him.

BRIGAND  
The woman. What was she?

Krem looks back at the hole Kara made in the bar wall.

KREM  
A mistake someone left in the sun too long.

He slots the recovered drive crystal into a portable cradle. The ship's engines deepen from sick cough to predatory hum.

BRIGAND  
We should finish her.

Krem sees Kara inside, kneeling over the dog instead of chasing him. He understands leverage when it presents itself.

KREM  
We already did something better.

He touches a small hardcase at his throat: the antidote dose on a chain, tucked beneath his armor.

KREM  
Go.

The Brigand ship lifts, tearing red dust into the air.

---

### INT. EVELIE PORT BAR — CONTINUOUS

Kara looks up as the ship's shadow passes the broken wall.

For one second, she could chase.

Krypto convulses.

The choice is made for her, or by her.

KARA  
No, no, stay with me.

Ruthye watches the ship vanish, then looks down at the tracker in her hand. Still glowing. Still a line.

RUTHYE  
I can still find him.

KARA  
Good. Then you can find him after we keep Krypto alive.

RUTHYE  
Your ship?

Kara scoops Krypto into her arms. He is too heavy for a human and heavier than grief.

KARA  
Closest thing I have to a hospital.

She staggers once under the red sun and the drink and the dog. Ruthye moves under one side of Krypto without being asked.

Kara looks at her.

RUTHYE  
I am not helping you abandon the hunt.

KARA  
Obviously not.

Together, they carry him out.

---

### INT. KARA'S SHIP — MEDBAY — LATER

Krypto lies on a narrow diagnostic slab. Kara works the controls with shaking hands. Ruthye stands nearby, still holding the tracker.

The medbay projects a rotating toxicology scan.

SHIP VOICE  
Foreign venom complex identified. Systemic collapse progressive. Recommended treatment: antidote match.

KARA  
Synthesize.

SHIP VOICE  
Unable.

KARA  
Broad-spectrum neutralizer.

SHIP VOICE  
Insufficient.

KARA  
Stasis.

SHIP VOICE  
Temporary stabilization possible. Irreversible collapse window remains active.

Numbers appear. They are not friendly.

RUTHYE  
What does it say?

KARA  
It says I should have left this planet a week ago.

RUTHYE  
Can sunlight heal him?

Kara looks to the red sun through the port.

KARA  
Not that one.

She opens a cabinet and yanks out an emergency kit. Half the compartments are empty. One contains a child's drawing of Krypto taped over old Kryptonian instructions.

Ruthye notices the drawing.

RUTHYE  
Who made that?

Kara takes too long.

KARA  
Someone who thought I answered messages.

She loads a stabilizer ampule into the medbay with more force than precision.

SHIP VOICE  
Warning. Improper dosage may induce cardiac—

KARA  
He's Kryptonian.

SHIP VOICE  
Species profile corrupted.

KARA  
Everything is corrupted.

Ruthye steps to a loose panel and presses it closed before it can spark again. Competent without being asked.

Kara sees this. Files it away.

RUTHYE  
Tell me what to hold.

KARA  
You know ships?

RUTHYE  
I know when things are about to fall.

Kara points to a manual pressure valve.

KARA  
When that needle hits red, pull down and don't let go until I say.

RUTHYE  
And if you do not say?

Kara looks at Krypto.

KARA  
Use judgment.

Ruthye grips the valve.

The needle crawls toward red.

She pulls.

The medbay steadies by a fraction.

KARA  
Good.

RUTHYE  
Do not sound surprised.

KARA  
I am surprised by everything still working.

She moves to the pilot controls.

### INT. KARA'S SHIP — COCKPIT — CONTINUOUS

Kara straps in. Ruthye follows, wiping chemical foam from her hands. She does not know where to stand.

KARA  
Hold onto something that looks expensive.

RUTHYE  
Why expensive?

KARA  
Because I probably bolted it down better.

Ruthye grabs the back of Kara's chair.

KARA  
Not me. I am famously under-maintained.

The ship launches hard.

Red atmosphere burns away. Stars open.

For half a second Ruthye forgets Krem, forgets the code, forgets to be armored. Her first sky gives way to the universe.

Then Krypto cries out from medbay and the armor returns.

Kara punches coordinates.

RUTHYE  
Where are we going?

KARA  
Closest yellow sun.

RUTHYE  
Krem has the antidote.

KARA  
Sun first.

RUTHYE  
My tracker—

KARA  
Sun first.

Ruthye looks back toward medbay.

KARA  
Under red light, my people get weak. Under yellow light, we heal. If I can get him close enough, maybe he can burn through it.

RUTHYE  
Maybe.

KARA  
Maybe is better than watching him die on the floor because I wanted to feel less.

Ruthye has no reply to that.

Warning lights bloom.

SHIP VOICE  
Drive temperature beyond recommended threshold.

KARA  
Recommendations are for people with options.

SHIP VOICE  
Fuel reserves critical.

KARA  
I know.

SHIP VOICE  
Jump coil destabilizing.

KARA  
I know.

The manual valve in the medbay alarm-pings.

KARA  
Valve!

Ruthye runs back.

### INT. KARA'S SHIP — MEDBAY / COCKPIT — INTERCUT

Ruthye braces both feet against the floor and pulls the valve down again. The ship bucks. Krypto's tube flashes amber.

RUTHYE  
It is not staying down!

KARA  
Wrap the strap through the lower ring!

RUTHYE  
What strap?

KARA  
Any strap!

Ruthye rips a belt from a storage hook, threads it through the valve, and leans back with all her weight.

Kara reroutes power in the cockpit, one hand flying over controls, the other white-knuckled on the throttle.

KARA  
Good. Hold that.

RUTHYE  
That was already the plan.

Krypto whimpers.

Kara pushes harder.

Ruthye watches her from the medbay doorway: this adult, panicking, failing, trying anyway.

RUTHYE  
You will break the ship.

KARA  
Ships can be fixed.

RUTHYE  
Can dogs?

The question lands. Kara has no answer.

A violent shudder. The stars smear, then snap back.

SHIP VOICE  
Emergency drop initiated.

Kara slams the console.

KARA  
No, no, no—

The ship drops out near a massive deep-space structure glowing with signs, docks, tubes, and traffic lanes.

A REST STOP. A transit hub. A fluorescent miracle in the void.

SHIP VOICE  
Nearest certified repair and medical facility reached.

Kara looks at it.

KARA  
Certified. Great.

---

### INT. DEEP-SPACE REST STOP — DOCK MEDBAY — LATER

Krypto floats in a liquid stabilizing tube. Tubes glow. Monitors pulse.

A DOCK HEALER, many-eyed and practical, reviews the scan.

DOCK HEALER  
Brigand poison.

KARA  
Can you cure it?

DOCK HEALER  
No.

KARA  
Can anyone here cure it?

DOCK HEALER  
Also no, but with more steps.

Kara closes her eyes.

DOCK HEALER  
The good news: he is large and stubborn.

KARA  
He is the best dog in the universe.

DOCK HEALER  
That is not a medical category, but it does appear relevant.

Ruthye watches Krypto's paw twitch in the fluid.

RUTHYE  
Who carries the antidote?

DOCK HEALER  
Ranking Brigands. Their weapons kill their own as efficiently as everyone else.

RUTHYE  
Krem.

Kara looks at her.

RUTHYE  
He carries it.

Kara touches the glass.

KARA  
Hold him.

DOCK HEALER  
We can slow the collapse. Hours. Not days.

KARA  
Hold him.

DOCK HEALER  
That is what I said, but with less optimism.

Kara stays at the tube after the healer leaves. The rest stop hums beyond the glass: announcements, engines, alien laughter, life continuing rudely around catastrophe.

Kara presses two fingers to the tube.

KARA  
I'm coming back.

Krypto's paw twitches near her fingers.

KARA  
Do not make me explain to Clark that I lost the only member of this family with social skills.

Ruthye stands behind her.

RUTHYE  
Who is Clark?

Kara takes a moment before answering.

KARA  
Family.

RUTHYE  
Does he wait for you too?

Kara does not look away from Krypto.

KARA  
Yes.

RUTHYE  
And you do not go to him.

It is not an accusation. Worse: it is accurate.

KARA  
No.

Ruthye absorbs this. A living family left waiting. The mirror turns, just a little.

---

### INT. REST STOP — REPAIR BAY

A MECHANIC stands beneath Kara's ship, unimpressed.

MECHANIC  
You burned the jump coil.

KARA  
Can you replace it?

MECHANIC  
Regrow it.

KARA  
Regrow.

MECHANIC  
Yes.

KARA  
How long?

MECHANIC  
If the vat likes you? Days. If the vat hates you? Also days, but with attitude.

Kara looks toward the medbay.

KARA  
I don't have days.

MECHANIC  
Then you don't have a ship.

Kara looks at the open service bay: her ship suspended in clamps, gutted and helpless, the closest thing she has to a home reduced to parts.

KARA  
Can you at least make sure it doesn't leave without me?

MECHANIC  
It could not leave if it hated you personally.

KARA  
It might.

MECHANIC  
Then I will restrain it emotionally.

---

### INT. REST STOP — CONCOURSE

The rest stop is riotous life: alien snacks, directional signs in fifteen alphabets, a tiny creature angrily pulling luggage twice its size, a kiosk selling self-deploying bubble suits, vending machines selling food that appears to object to being food.

Kara moves through it with weary competence. This is not strange to her. She knows which customs queue is fake, which vendor waters down the electrolyte broth, which floor stripe means private atmosphere and which means legal hazard.

Ruthye is trying very hard not to stare at everything.

KARA  
You can look.

RUTHYE  
I am looking.

KARA  
You're doing the warrior version. It's making the pastry clerk nervous.

A PASTRY CLERK with six eyes looks away too fast.

Kara buys two skewers of something steaming blue.

RUTHYE  
What is it?

KARA  
Breakfast.

RUTHYE  
It is afternoon.

KARA  
Space.

Ruthye sniffs the skewer.

KARA  
If it whistles, don't bite the end with eyes.

Ruthye studies Kara for the joke. Kara gives nothing away.

Ruthye takes a careful bite. The skewer emits a tiny melodic note.

RUTHYE  
It whistled.

KARA  
Good. Fresh.

For the first time, Ruthye almost laughs. Almost.

They pass a family of travelers: two parents trying to keep three children from climbing into a display fountain. Ruthye slows without meaning to.

Kara sees. Says nothing.

---

### INT. REST STOP — INFORMATION DESK

Kara buys two tickets with the resigned pain of someone spending the last of her money.

Ruthye places her tracker on the counter beside a public spacemap.

The ATTENDANT watches the tracker line up with route grids.

ATTENDANT  
You don't want to go there.

KARA  
What is there?

The attendant taps the map.

ATTENDANT  
Bilki.

RUTHYE  
What is Bilki?

ATTENDANT  
Was. Depending who you ask.

KARA  
Was what?

ATTENDANT  
Brigand homeworld. Travel advisory. Toxic atmosphere, structural collapse, active automated defenses, radiation storms, cultural trauma, no refunds.

RUTHYE  
Is it still on the drop-off route?

The attendant hates this.

ATTENDANT  
Technically.

Ruthye looks to Kara.

Kara, despite herself, gives a goofy little grin.

KARA  
Technically is how most of my plans survive infancy.

ATTENDANT  
There is no scheduled pickup.

KARA  
Less good.

RUTHYE  
We only need to arrive.

ATTENDANT  
Everybody says that going in.

Kara notices Ruthye eyeing a display: a self-deploying bubble space-suit ring.

Kara grabs one and adds it to the purchase.

RUTHYE  
I did not ask for that.

KARA  
Now you won't die in space.

RUTHYE  
Was that likely?

KARA  
On public transit? Eventually.

---

### INT. REST STOP — WASHROOM

Bright mirrors. Strange sinks. A sign with too many instructions.

Kara and Ruthye clean blood and grime from their faces.

For the first time, Ruthye looks small. A child in a too-bright room very far from home.

Kara sees it in the mirror.

A tear slides down Ruthye's cheek before Ruthye can stop it.

Kara reaches out and wipes it away with her thumb.

No speech.

Ruthye lets it happen for one second.

Then the armor returns.

RUTHYE  
The bus leaves soon.

KARA  
Yeah.

RUTHYE  
You should eat something.

KARA  
You should be thirteen.

RUTHYE  
I was.

She exits.

Kara looks at herself. At the red in her eyes. At the jacket hanging off her like a shelter.

She follows.

### INT. INTERGALACTIC BUS — DEPARTURE

### INT. INTERGALACTIC BUS — LATER

Kara jerks awake from another fragment: Argo, alarms, her mother's mouth forming words without sound.

Ruthye watches.

RUTHYE  
You should stop that.

KARA

RUTHYE  
Good.

KARA  
My world died slowly.

Ruthye does not turn from the window, but she listens.

KARA  
Not like stories. Stories make endings clean because people telling them need mercy. It wasn't clean. The sky went wrong first. Then the ground. Then systems failed in boring ways. Food. Heat. Doors. People died in ones and twos and then too many to count.

KARA

You obeyed?

RUTHYE  
Did you?

KARA  
Every day.

Stars pass.

KARA  
I've been down your road. Revenge won't make you feel better.

RUTHYE  
Did yours deserve it?

RUTHYE  
The person you killed.

KARA  
Yes.

RUTHYE  
Then it was justice.

KARA  
Maybe.

RUTHYE  
Maybe?

KARA  
He was dangerous. He had done terrible things. If I told you the story clean, you would say I was right.

RUTHYE  
Were you?

Kara looks at her hands.

KARA  
That is the worst part.

RUTHYE  
What is?

KARA  
Sometimes being right has nothing to do with what it leaves in you.

RUTHYE  
So we do nothing.

KARA  
No. In a fight, when someone is coming for a life, you stop them. Sometimes stopping them kills them. That isn't clean either, but the world is not clean.

RUTHYE  
Then what is forbidden?

Kara struggles with the simplicity of the question.

KARA  
The moment after. When they cannot hurt anyone and you still want to make them pay. The cold part.

Ruthye absorbs that. Does not accept it. Not yet.

RUTHYE  
My father deserves justice.

KARA  
Yes.

RUTHYE  
Krem deserves death.

KARA  
I know.

KARA  
I want him dead too.

RUTHYE  
Then why stop me?

Kara looks out at the beauty and emptiness.

KARA  
Because you deserve to come home.

Ruthye's reflection hardens in the glass.

RUTHYE  
You say that like it is simple.

KARA  
No.

RUTHYE  
My brother was in my arms. He was shaking. I could feel his teeth hit each other. My mother was on the floor. My father was—

She stops. The bus hum fills the gap.

RUTHYE  
If I go home without doing what the law demands, then the house stays like that forever.

KARA  
It already happened.

RUTHYE  
I know.

KARA  
Killing him won't unmake the room.

Ruthye finally looks at her.

RUTHYE  
Then what does?

Kara has no answer that would not be a lie.

KARA  
Nothing.

That is worse than comfort. It is honest.

Ruthye turns away.

### EXT. ABOVE BILKI — CONTINUOUS

Ruthye notices.

Close.

### EXT. BILKI SURFACE — DEAD WORLD

Kara and Ruthye step into nuclear winter.

No birds. No color. Ruins like ribs.

Ruthye checks the tracker.

RUTHYE  
This way.

They move through old Brigand infrastructure: depots, transit spines, collapsed towers, signs for a civilization that kept working after it stopped living.

A schoolyard lies under ash. A transit sign still flickers arrival times for trains that will never come. On a wall, a mural of Bilki before the winter: oceans, towers, children with painted hands.

Kara slows.

For a moment Bilki is Argo. Another dead world. Another place where the sky failed.

She reaches into her jacket and finds the flask by muscle memory.

Ruthye sees.

RUTHYE  
No.

RUTHYE  
Not here.

KARA  
You giving orders now?

RUTHYE  
Yes.

KARA  
Good.

KARA  
Down!

RUTHYE  
I can move.

KARA  
I know.

RUTHYE  
Then let me.

When bullets start respecting your code, absolutely.

RUTHYE  
Put me down!

KARA  
Busy!

RUTHYE  
I did not come here to be carried!

KARA  
Nobody comes anywhere to be carried!

A perimeter gun unfolds ahead, targeting Ruthye's small heat signature. Ruthye sees it and twists to draw her sword.

KARA  
Don't you dare sword the gun.

RUTHYE  
I can reach the hinge.

KARA  
It has bullets. From far away. That is the gun's whole argument.

Kara throws a chunk of concrete. It hits the gun hard enough to dent it, not hard enough to destroy it. The gun re-centers.

Kara plants herself in front of Ruthye and takes the burst across her shoulder and back.

She screams through her teeth but does not move.

RUTHYE  
Kara!

KARA  
Now the hinge.

Ruthye darts under Kara's arm, cuts the exposed servo, and the gun collapses sparking.

RUTHYE  
I told you.

KARA  
Yes. You're very hinge-aware. Move.

Another blast. Kara turns her body to shield Ruthye. The leather jacket shreds further.

Kara stops.

KARA  
That laugh better belong to someone on our side.

RUTHYE  
Do we have a side?

KARA  
Increasingly unclear.

They move toward the carnage.

### EXT. BILKI DEPOT — CONTINUOUS

LOBO is there.

Huge. Pale. Grinning. Chain flashing. Blue bike parked in the smoke like an insult.

He tears through Brigands with obscene ease. A body goes one way, a weapon another. He is not fighting a battle so much as enjoying a chore.

A Brigand fires into Lobo's chest. The rounds flatten and fall.

BRIGAND  
Monster.

LOBO  
Don't flirt unless you mean it.

BRIGAND  
Please—

LOBO  
You're welcome.

Kara and Ruthye emerge at the edge of the depot.

Lobo lifts a heavy transport truck overhead, silhouetted by flame like an icon.

Main Man, one. Assorted idiots, not one.

Kara stares.

KARA  
Lobo.

RUTHYE  
You know him?

KARA  
Everybody knows him eventually. Usually briefly.

KREM  
Listen—

LOBO  
No.

KARA  
Wait—

Ruthye steps forward at the same time.

RUTHYE  
You can't kill him.

LOBO  
Come again?

Ruthye.

...Huh.

No.

Why?
