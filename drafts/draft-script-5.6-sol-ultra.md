# SUPERGIRL

FADE IN:

**1. EXT. KNOLL HOMESTEAD - RED EVENING**

A red sun hangs over fields of black grain.

Not sunset. This is simply the color of the day here — rust on the irrigation vanes, ember-light along the stone walls, a low red shine caught in every window of a small homestead on a grassy knoll.

A six-year-old boy tears through the grain with a paper kite. TAVI, all knees and stubborn joy. The kite is shaped like a many-winged farm bird and flies badly.

RUTHYE, thirteen, follows at a dignified distance, carrying Tavi's boots and pretending not to be part of this.

The kite dives. Tavi dives after it.

Ruthye catches him by the back of his tunic before he lands face-first in an irrigation trench.

TAVI

I had it.

RUTHYE

You had the ground.

TAVI

It was in my keeping.

RUTHYE

It was trying to escape you.

She sets him upright. Hands him his boots.

Their FATHER stands in the kitchen doorway, sleeves rolled, flour on both forearms. Their MOTHER appears behind him with a hot pie and uses her hip to move him aside.

MOTHER

Wash. Both of you.

Tavi bolts inside.

Ruthye retrieves the torn kite from the grain. One strut has snapped. She tests it with careful fingers.

FATHER

Can it be saved?

RUTHYE

Obviously.

FATHER

Then I withdraw the question.

He waits for her. They go in together.

Far down the red road, three pinpricks of light crest a hill.

**2. INT. HOMESTEAD KITCHEN - RED EVENING**

The room was built by many hands over many years. Repaired chairs. Uneven cabinets. A long table polished by sleeves and elbows.

The family eats stew. Steam clouds the windows.

Tavi palms a roasted root from Ruthye's plate. Ruthye catches his wrist without looking.

RUTHYE

Debt.

TAVI

I fed the south calf.

RUTHYE

That was your assigned labor.

TAVI

It was choking.

MOTHER

It was.

TAVI

A life in your keeping comes before a debt.

He recites it proudly, then eats the root.

RUTHYE

You memorized one useful line.

FATHER

Most law is one useful line surrounded by people explaining why it does not apply to them.

Mother cuts the pie. Tavi's portion is structurally impossible.

RUTHYE

That is larger than mine.

MOTHER

He saved a life.

RUTHYE

The calf coughed once.

TAVI

Violently.

Father slides a sliver of his own piece onto Ruthye's plate.

FATHER

Justice.

Three SHADOWS pass across the steamed window.

Nobody at the table moves.

A KNOCK at the door. Soft. Almost courteous.

Father wipes his hands and opens it.

KREM stands outside with THREE BRIGANDS.

Travel grime. Layered armor. Weapons made to survive neglect. Krem's hair and beard have gone silver without making him look old. Around his neck hangs a squat vial in an armored metal cage.

KREM

Good evening.

His eyes move over the table, the exits, the family.

KREM (CONT'D)

We have had trouble on the road.

FATHER

The port is west.

KREM

Yes.

Krem steps over the threshold. His men follow.

KREM (CONT'D)

That is where the trouble began.

One Brigand closes the door behind him.

Krem removes his gloves finger by finger. Sits in Father's chair.

The Brigands do not point their weapons. They do not need to. One takes up position near the back door. Another looks through the hall. The third places a scarred equipment satchel beside the table and remains standing.

Krem looks at the pie.

KREM (CONT'D)

May I?

He has already taken Father's fork.

Mother stands very still.

MOTHER

You are in our home.

KREM

I am aware of it.

He cuts himself a piece from the center, ruining every remaining slice.

KREM (CONT'D)

Weapons. Field cells. Preserved food. Medical supplies if you keep them. We will leave you enough for the week.

FATHER

There are children here.

KREM

Then you will understand why I have been specific.

He eats. Considers.

KREM (CONT'D)

This is very good.

Tavi looks to Ruthye. She puts one hand under the table, palm open. He takes it.

Father measures the room. Three Brigands. Mother. Two children.

FATHER

The field armory is through the washroom. Outside door.

The SECOND BRIGAND gestures with his weapon.

SECOND BRIGAND

Show me.

FATHER

I can tell you what is there.

KREM

He asked to see it.

Father gets up.

Ruthye starts to rise. Father's look stops her.

**3. INT./EXT. HOMESTEAD / FIELD ARMORY - CONTINUOUS**

Father leads the Second and Third Brigands through the washroom and out to a lean-to built against the house.

The Second Brigand opens racks and calls inventory into a wrist unit.

SECOND BRIGAND

Six field cells. Two long guns. Cutter. Farm charges.

THIRD BRIGAND

Pistol.

He lifts a compact FIELD PISTOL with a white grip. An inventory tag blinks beneath the trigger guard.

FATHER

That stays.

The Third Brigand tucks it into his belt.

THIRD BRIGAND

Then remember it fondly.

Inside, Krem eats pie while Mother and Ruthye watch him.

KREM

Do you know Bilki?

MOTHER

No.

KREM

You do.

He nods toward the stamped metal plate on their stove. A manufacturer's mark, old and nearly scrubbed away.

KREM (CONT'D)

You simply know it as something you bought cheaply.

Mother looks at the mark. Ruthye does too.

KREM (CONT'D)

We made things that lasted. Pumps. Pressure doors. Heat exchangers. The kind of objects people stop seeing because they work.

The First Brigand leaves his equipment satchel by Ruthye's chair and moves into the pantry. He begins pulling down jars.

KREM (CONT'D)

When our northern states burned, our neighbors sold fuel to both sides. When the sky turned black, they closed their ports so our sickness would remain ours.

He says it without heat. A remembered list.

KREM (CONT'D)

Evelie sold field capacitors. Guidance glass. Agricultural compounds that became something else when mixed by educated men.

Father comes back in behind the armed Brigands.

FATHER

My family sold nothing to anyone on Bilki.

Krem glances at the stove plate again.

FATHER (CONT'D)

Our people were not part of that.

The lie is small. Perhaps inherited. It still changes the air.

Krem lets it sit.

KREM

No. Of course not.

The Third Brigand sets the white-gripped pistol on the table while he pulls empty sacks from the satchel.

Inside the open satchel: a baseball-sized case of grungy metal and thick glass. At its heart, something amber pulses like a seed trying to germinate.

Ruthye sees the Brigands. Sees the case.

Tavi drops his spoon.

It CLATTERS beneath the table.

TAVI

Sorry.

Ruthye ducks to retrieve it.

Under cover of the table, her other hand slips into the satchel. The case is heavier than she expects. She draws it into the folds of her skirt.

Tavi sees.

Ruthye puts one finger to her lips.

Above them, Krem continues.

KREM

I was proud once. That is the embarrassing part. I believed good work was protection. I believed law was a wall.

Ruthye rises with the spoon. The cased JUMP-SEED has vanished.

KREM (CONT'D)

Then I watched the walls become inventory.

He takes another bite.

KREM (CONT'D)

Afterward, the people who survived learned a simpler economy.

RUTHYE

Taking.

Krem looks at her for the first time.

KREM

Continuing.

RUTHYE

They are not the same.

KREM

Not while the pantry is full.

**4. INT. HOMESTEAD KITCHEN - CONTINUOUS**

Mother lays a hand on Ruthye's shoulder.

MOTHER

The supplies are yours. Take them and leave.

The Brigands have packed the weapons and food. The Second Brigand gives Krem a nod: enough.

Krem wipes Father's fork on Father's napkin.

KREM

Your hospitality does you credit.

He rises.

Mother reaches for the pie, to move it out of his way or reclaim one inch of the table.

Krem STRIKES her.

Not a killing blow. A flat, economical backhand.

Mother hits the cabinet. Tavi screams.

Father is on Krem before thought can catch him.

The Brigands seize Father. One drives a knee into him. Another clubs him across the back. The table flips. Bowls shatter. Tavi crawls beneath a chair.

Ruthye reaches for the white-gripped pistol.

The Third Brigand gets it first and jams it against Father's temple.

THIRD BRIGAND

Still.

The room goes still.

Krem touches his split lip. Looks at the blood on his thumb.

Father is forced to his knees, panting. Mother pushes herself upright, one hand braced on the cabinet. Ruthye stands in front of Tavi.

Krem looks from Father to Mother.

KREM

Enough.

The pistol lowers.

KREM (CONT'D)

He protected his wife. I would think less of him if he had not.

Krem retrieves his gloves. Pulls one on. Then the other.

KREM (CONT'D)

We have what we came for.

The Brigands release Father.

He remains on his knees, alive.

The Second Brigand shoulders the sack of weapons. The First carries the food. The Third pockets the white-gripped pistol.

Krem walks to the open door.

Red evening spills across the wrecked kitchen. Outside: the road. Their waiting transport. Departure.

Krem crosses the threshold.

Father crawls to Mother. She grips his hand.

Tavi begins to cry into the back of Ruthye's dress.

It is over.

Then Krem looks back.

Ruthye is staring at him.

No tears. No lowered eyes. Her body shields her brother, but her face says she is recording every detail for a future court.

Krem knows that face.

For one beat, grief shows in his own.

Then he makes a choice.

KREM (CONT'D)

The pistol.

The Third Brigand hands him the white-gripped weapon.

Father understands before anyone else.

FATHER

No. Not in front of—

Krem FIRES.

Father folds beside Mother's chair.

Krem never looks away from Ruthye.

KREM

This is what the world is.

He sets the pistol into his own belt and leaves.

The Brigands follow. Their footsteps recede.

**5. INT. HOMESTEAD KITCHEN - MOMENTS LATER**

No one in the kitchen moves until an engine starts beyond the grain.

Then Mother is on the floor beside Father.

Ruthye turns, catches Tavi's head in both hands before he can look.

RUTHYE

Eyes here.

He tries to turn.

RUTHYE (CONT'D)

Tavi. Eyes here.

He looks at her. She pulls him against her, his face buried beneath her chin.

Behind her, Mother makes one low, ruined sound.

The cased jump-seed presses hard against Ruthye's ribs. She reaches inside her dress and closes a hand around it.

Through the open door, the Brigands' lights disappear along the red road.

Ruthye holds the life in her arms.

And stares after the death she has chosen to follow.

CUT TO:

**6. INT. KARA'S SHIP - DREAM**

Black.

A CITY GROANS around her.

White towers buckle beneath a sky webbed with fire.

A GIRL'S HAND beats against curved glass.

KARA'S MOTHER, on the other side — her face streaked with dust, her mouth forming words we cannot hear.

KARA'S FATHER pulls at a failing control.

A violent FLASH —

The city shears away into stars.

Kara reaches for both of them. The distance grows faster than her arm.

Her mother's palm against the glass.

Her father's mouth shapes one last syllable —

Silence.

**7. INT. KARA'S SHIP - MORNING**

Kara wakes with her hand outstretched.

Her eyes remain shut.

The same hand finds a bottle on the deck.

She drinks before she opens them.

KARA ZOR-EL lies half on a narrow bunk, half off it. Boots on. A faded House of El travel suit creased from sleep. Her battered leather jacket serves as blanket, pillow and barricade.

The cabin around her has gone from lived-in to neglected by slow degrees. Tools left in open panels. A cup growing a small blue fungus. A maintenance light blinking with the patience of something long ignored.

Kara opens her eyes.

The red sun fills the porthole.

KRYPTO sits beside the bunk, watching her. White fur. Ears too expressive for her present dignity.

KARA

Don't.

Krypto tilts his head.

KARA (CONT'D)

I know what time it is.

The ship CHIMES.

SHIP

Three messages held. One marked urgent. Drive maintenance overdue.

KARA

None of those sound like me problems.

SHIP

Today is Kara Zor-El's birthday.

Kara closes her eyes again.

KARA

Traitor.

Krypto nudges the bottle away with his nose.

KARA (CONT'D)

You live here for free.

He puts one paw on her chest.

KARA (CONT'D)

Fine.

She sits up too fast. Waits for the cabin to stop tilting. The red sun has taken the effortless grace out of her; the alcohol has taken most of what remains.

She taps the message console.

A small audio waveform appears. CLARK'S recorded voice is warm, careful, and trying not to sound worried.

CLARK (RECORDING)

Hey, Kara. Happy birthday.

Kara reaches for the bottle. Krypto's paw pins it.

CLARK (RECORDING)

I know you wanted to be off-world this year. I know why. I'm not asking you to come back today.

A faint kitchen CLATTER sounds on his end.

CLARK (RECORDING)

Lois made a cake. It has become a geological event. We're monitoring it.

A faint smile almost reaches Kara. Almost.

CLARK (RECORDING)

Just call me. You don't have to make it a birthday call. You don't have to say anything useful. Let me know you're there.

His practiced lightness slips in the recording.

CLARK (RECORDING)

You don't have to do this one alone.

The message ends.

The console offers: RETURN CALL.

Kara looks at it.

Krypto looks at her.

Kara dismisses the prompt.

KARA

Breakfast.

She swings her jacket on over the faded suit. Too large, too worn, familiar enough to hide in.

At the food unit, she shakes a container. Empty. Tries another.

SHIP

Primary drive inspection overdue by four hundred and seventeen standard days.

KARA

Put it on the list.

SHIP

It is the list.

Kara finds one protein strip. Krypto accepts it with undeserved enthusiasm.

She scratches behind his ears. Her forehead rests against his for half a second.

KARA

We'll get real food.

Krypto thumps his tail.

KARA (CONT'D)

And I will get something that isn't.

She takes the bottle. Opens the hatch.

Dead red light pours in.

**8. EXT. KNOLL HOMESTEAD - MORNING**

Flat stones rise in a new cairn above the black-grain field.

Father lies beneath them.

Mother places the last stone. One cheek is dark with bruising. Tavi stands against her side, holding the repaired paper kite by its broken-wing tail.

Ruthye stands apart in travel clothes, a narrow sword belted at her hip. A satchel crosses her body. Deep inside it, the cased jump-seed gives off a muffled amber pulse.

Mother sees the satchel.

MOTHER

No.

RUTHYE

He is still on Evelie.

MOTHER

No.

RUTHYE

His transport flew west. The port is west.

MOTHER

The wardens have been called.

RUTHYE

From the basin. They will arrive after he leaves.

MOTHER

Then he leaves.

Ruthye absorbs that as if Mother has proposed abandoning Father beneath open sky.

RUTHYE

Blood taken outside the heat of battle is answered by the eldest capable hand.

MOTHER

You have selected the line that gives your anger a weapon.

RUTHYE

It is the Code.

MOTHER

So is the rest of it.

Mother steps close.

MOTHER (CONT'D)

A life in your keeping comes before debt.

Ruthye glances at Tavi.

RUTHYE

He is in yours.

MOTHER

He was in yours when you held him.

That lands. Ruthye's face does not move.

MOTHER (CONT'D)

He is still asking why you would not let him turn around.

Tavi looks down at the kite.

RUTHYE

Because he should not have seen.

MOTHER

Neither should you.

RUTHYE

I did.

Mother reaches for her. Ruthye lets herself be held, but only for a breath.

MOTHER

What did you take from them?

Ruthye's eyes flick to the satchel.

MOTHER (CONT'D)

I know my daughter.

RUTHYE

Their jump-seed. They can cross the county without it. They cannot clear the system.

MOTHER

Give it to the wardens.

RUTHYE

When they arrive.

MOTHER

Give it to me.

Ruthye steps back.

RUTHYE

They took Father's white-gripped pistol. Its inventory tag is still live.

She produces a palm-sized FARM INVENTORY READER. On its scratched screen: a simple white grip icon and a weak directional line pointing west.

RUTHYE (CONT'D)

They are at the port now. I can keep them here until the wardens come.

MOTHER

That is not what you mean to do.

RUTHYE

It is what the Code permits.

MOTHER

Ruthye.

RUTHYE

It is what it demands.

Tavi crosses the space between them. Holds out the kite.

TAVI

For the road.

Ruthye looks at the absurd paper bird.

RUTHYE

It will not survive the road.

TAVI

You fixed it.

She takes it. Folds it carefully and slides it beneath the flap of her satchel, over the stolen seed.

TAVI (CONT'D)

Are you coming back?

Ruthye kneels so they are eye to eye.

RUTHYE

Stay with Mother. Lock the lower door before dark. The north hinge catches, so lift as you pull.

TAVI

That wasn't my question.

Ruthye cannot give him a lie cleanly enough.

She kisses his forehead. Stands.

Mother blocks the road for one long beat.

Then she steps aside — not permission. An admission that force would only make Ruthye sneak away later.

MOTHER

Every line, Ruthye. Not only the one that hurts like you do.

Ruthye walks down from the cairn.

She does not look back.

Tavi does.

**9. EXT. EVELIE ROAD - DAY**

Ruthye walks west under the red sun.

The road threads through low farms, abandoned ore cuts, villages built around shade wells. Freight crawlers throw dust over her boots. She never asks for a ride.

The inventory reader wakes in her palm every few hundred steps.

Weak pulse. White grip icon. West.

At a fork, the line shifts. Ruthye shifts with it.

She drinks from a small canteen. Counts what remains. Keeps moving.

At the top of the last ridge, the land falls away into EVELIE PORT — a crowded basin of launch towers, repair gantries and low buildings that have been sandblasted the same color as the ground.

A small Brigand transport cuts across the basin on local thrusters. It can still fly.

It circles back toward the port.

It cannot leave.

Ruthye touches the hidden jump-seed through her satchel.

Below, the tracker line sharpens.

**10. EXT. EVELIE PORT - LATE DAY**

Ruthye passes shipbreakers and fuel stalls. Dockhands in pressure scarves. Long-haul crews blinking against gravity after months away.

She has never seen this many kinds of person in one place. She gives none of them the satisfaction of noticing.

The signal leads toward a low building under a failing sign:

PORT OF CALL.

Someone has shot out the word CALL.

**11. INT. PORT BAR - LATE DAY**

Red light presses through dirty windows. Inside, it loses a territorial dispute with green lamps, blue vapor and a ceiling fan that turns one blade short.

The room serves pilots, harvest crews and people who prefer not to say what they do. A stone-skinned card dealer sheds pebbles when he bluffs. A translucent patron keeps her drink inside her chest. Nobody comments.

Kara occupies a back booth with a bottle, a chipped glass and Krypto under the table.

The battered jacket swallows the faded House of El suit. Her hair has been tied back and has escaped in stages. She is not celebrating.

The BARTENDER sets down a plate for Krypto.

BARTENDER

That is the last of the smoked runner.

KARA

He said thank you.

BARTENDER

He looked at you.

KARA

He has a complicated accent.

Krypto eats. Kara does not.

The door opens. Ruthye steps in.

The room notices the sword first, then her age, then the fact that she meets every look.

Ruthye checks the inventory reader. The directional line sweeps across the room, uncertain among walls and machinery.

A broad PORT RUFFIAN moves his chair into her path.

PORT RUFFIAN

Minors surrender steel at the bar.

RUTHYE

Move.

PORT RUFFIAN

House rule.

BARTENDER

It is not.

The Ruffian smiles without looking back.

PORT RUFFIAN

New rule.

He reaches for Ruthye's sword.

She catches his thumb, turns under his arm and drives his wrist flat onto the nearest table. His own knife comes out of his sleeve into her free hand.

He tries to rise. Ruthye shifts one inch. His elbow approaches a direction it was not designed to take.

He sits.

RUTHYE

You have surrendered steel at the table.

She lays his knife beyond his reach.

A few patrons look away to hide smiles.

The Ruffian's other hand drops toward a holstered pulse gun.

Under Kara's table, Krypto stops eating.

Kara watches over the rim of her glass.

Ruthye sees the movement in the reflection of the Ruffian's metal cup. She kicks his chair leg. He topples backward before he clears leather.

His pulse gun skitters across the floor and comes to rest beneath Kara's boot.

Kara looks down at it.

KARA

No.

She pushes it toward the bartender.

KARA (CONT'D)

You're making the dog anxious.

Krypto returns to his plate.

The Bartender points at the Ruffian.

BARTENDER

Outside.

PORT RUFFIAN

She started—

Every face in the room tells him how this sentence ends.

He retrieves his pride in several pieces and leaves without the knife or gun.

Ruthye looks around for the person least entertained.

Her eyes settle on Kara.

She crosses to the booth.

RUTHYE

I require muscle.

Kara looks behind herself, as if muscle might be sitting in the next booth.

KARA

Try the gentleman who just left.

RUTHYE

He has poor judgment.

KARA

That's most of the muscle market.

RUTHYE

You watched the exits before you watched the fight.

Kara pours another drink. Her hand misses the glass by half an inch. She corrects without acknowledging it.

KARA

You held a stranger's arm backward in a public house.

RUTHYE

He reached for me.

KARA

Everyone has a reason.

RUTHYE

Do you hire out?

KARA

No.

RUTHYE

Do you fight?

KARA

Also no.

The Bartender sets the confiscated pulse gun behind the bar.

BARTENDER

She fights.

Kara gives him a look.

BARTENDER (CONT'D)

Badly, lately.

Ruthye sits opposite Kara without invitation. She props her satchel between her boots.

Krypto noses out from under the table and studies her. Ruthye holds still while he smells the dust of the homestead on her hands.

KARA

He charges by the hour.

RUTHYE

I have no money.

KARA

Then he likes you already.

Ruthye scratches Krypto once beneath the chin. Precise. He leans his full weight into her knee.

Kara notices the dried blood beneath Ruthye's thumbnail.

KARA (CONT'D)

How old are you?

RUTHYE

Thirteen.

KARA

No.

RUTHYE

It was not a proposal.

KARA

Whatever you require muscle for, no.

Ruthye places the inventory reader on the table but keeps it off.

RUTHYE

A man named Krem came to my home yesterday. He stole our stores and weapons. He murdered my father.

Kara's glass stops halfway to her mouth.

RUTHYE (CONT'D)

Blood taken outside the heat of battle is answered by the eldest capable hand. I am the elder of two. I am capable.

KARA

That's a law?

RUTHYE

The Code.

KARA

And the Code sends thirteen-year-olds into port bars to hire drunks?

RUTHYE

It does not prohibit delegation.

Kara lets out one airless breath. Not quite a laugh.

KARA

Find a warden.

RUTHYE

The wardens are a day away. Krem is not.

KARA

Then let him be somewhere else.

RUTHYE

He killed my father.

KARA

I heard you.

RUTHYE

You do not appear to have.

Kara looks at her now. Fully.

KARA

Where's your family?

RUTHYE

My father is under stone.

KARA

The rest.

Ruthye hesitates. It is the first question that has made her do so.

RUTHYE

My mother is at our homestead. My brother is six.

Kara goes quiet.

The bar keeps moving around them. Glasses. Cards. The short fan clicking overhead.

KARA

Alive.

RUTHYE

Yes.

Kara looks down at Krypto. At the bottle. The red window beyond Ruthye.

KARA

Go home.

RUTHYE

No.

KARA

Your mother is alive. Your little brother is alive. They're standing in a house that just got torn open, and you left them there.

RUTHYE

My mother has him in her keeping.

KARA

That is a very tidy sentence.

RUTHYE

It is true.

Kara's composure splits, quietly and all at once.

KARA

My mother is dead.

Ruthye says nothing.

KARA (CONT'D)

My father too. The people who raised me. The streets, the rooms, the stupid places you hate until someone tells you they aren't there anymore.

Her thumb works at a gouge in the tabletop.

KARA (CONT'D)

You have a door you can still walk through.

RUTHYE

Then you understand why the man who took mine must answer.

KARA

No.

Kara meets her eyes.

KARA (CONT'D)

I understand why you should go home.

Ruthye's jaw tightens.

RUTHYE

I did not ask for counsel.

KARA

Good. I don't have any.

RUTHYE

I asked if you would help me.

KARA

And I said no.

Ruthye reaches into her satchel.

Kara's hand flashes across the table and closes around Ruthye's wrist.

Too fast for the state she is in. Too hard.

Ruthye winces.

Kara releases her immediately.

KARA (CONT'D)

Don't pull surprises in a room like this.

Ruthye studies Kara's hand. Recalculates.

Then she slowly removes the cased jump-seed and sets it on the table.

Amber light pulses through grungy glass.

Kara's drunkenness does not vanish. Her attention cuts through it.

KARA (CONT'D)

Where did you get that?

RUTHYE

From Krem.

KARA

You stole his jump-seed.

RUTHYE

I removed it from his keeping.

KARA

That's stealing with extra grammar.

RUTHYE

His transport can make a local hop. It cannot leave Evelie without this.

Kara examines the case without touching it.

KARA

So he isn't gone.

RUTHYE

I told you.

She places the inventory reader beside it.

RUTHYE (CONT'D)

This is separate. It is paired to a white-gripped field pistol from our armory. The inventory tag is still broadcasting.

Ruthye switches on the reader. A thin line searches across its screen.

She rotates it. The line brightens toward the bar's front wall.

Then toward the side.

She sets it flat and aligns its compass ring. The white grip icon BLOOMS.

The reader emits a hard, continuous tone.

RUTHYE

Close.

Krypto lifts his head.

The jump-seed answers with a different sound — three soft CHIRPS from inside its case.

Kara stares at it.

KARA

Turn it off.

Ruthye kills the inventory reader. The jump-seed chirps again.

KARA (CONT'D)

Not yours. Retrieval ping.

Kara grabs the case.

The bar door opens.

Krem stands there with three Brigands.

The white-gripped pistol rides in his belt. Its inventory tag flashes in time with Ruthye's dying screen.

Krem sees Ruthye.

Then the seed in Kara's hand.

KREM

There it is.

Ruthye rises.

Her chair slides back across the floor.

Krem steps inside. His men spread out, closing exits as naturally as breath. The armored vial at his neck catches the green bar light.

KREM (CONT'D)

You made us search half the county.

RUTHYE

You should have kept going.

Krem looks at the sword. At the dried blood under her nail. A trace of recognition, almost approval.

KREM

Give me the seed.

KARA

The kid leaves first.

Krem takes in Kara: bottle, battered jacket, unsteady stance.

KREM

This is not your concern.

KARA

**30. INT. REST STOP - TRANSIT MARKET - CONTINUOUS**

They move quickly through a market designed to stop them.

Every stall is a small impossibility.

An orchard grows upside down over one aisle, its glass fruit chiming when ripe. A vendor pours rain into folded paper. Children made of linked silver rings chase a warm ball of light between their parents' legs.

Ruthye tries to look only at the gate signs.

Her eyes refuse.

She has never seen a ceiling disappear into an indoor weather system. Never seen a fish swim through air from one bowl to another. Never seen six different kinds of sky sold in jars.

Kara sees her seeing it.

She does not smile. She just slows by half a step.

They pass a food cart. Flat blue cakes turn on a heated stone, opening like flowers at the edges. The smell catches Ruthye. She looks once and keeps walking.

Kara doubles back.

KARA

Two.

FOOD VENDOR

Salt.

She looks to Ruthye.

RUTHYE

What does weather taste like?

RUTHYE (CONT'D)

Salt.

Ruthye takes a careful bite.

Then the tracker pulses under her arm and her jaw tightens again.

They move.

The RING VENDOR is an elderly woman whose body occupies three adjacent moments. Each version of her reaches for a different tray.

RING VENDOR

Destination medium?

Vacuum, then toxic cold atmosphere. Child size.

RUTHYE

RING VENDOR

You are exactly child size.

RING VENDOR (CONT'D)

Emergency bubble. Pressure, warmth, six common gas blends. Manual deployment here. Automatic deployment under hard vacuum.

RING VOICE

DEPLOYING!

RING VENDOR

Do not deploy indoors unless death is otherwise immediate.

KARA

We'll try to respect the distinction.

KARA (CONT'D)

Now you won't die in space.

RUTHYE

Less likely now.

Ruthye tests the weight of the ring. She almost presses the green stud.

Kara closes Ruthye's fingers over it.

KARA (CONT'D)

RUTHYE

You need another jacket.

Kara looks down at the old leather.

KARA

This one still knows me.

STATION ANNOUNCER (V.O.)

Municipal service Eight-Fallow is boarding all recognized passengers and regrettable cargo.

KARA

Two minutes.

RUTHYE

You just said we had none.

KARA

I found some.

**31. INT. REST STOP - WASHROOM - CONTINUOUS**

A row of basins adjusts height as they approach.

Kara's rises. Ruthye's lowers.

Ruthye glares at hers until it rises two inches.

They stand side by side beneath mirrors that offer seventeen flattering spectra. Kara switches hers to plain reflection.

It is not flattering.

Her eyes are red. A bruise darkens one cheek. Dried blood has glued hair to her temple. Without motion to disguise it, she looks sick and exhausted.

Ruthye wets a cloth and wipes blood from her hand -- hers, a Brigand's, Krypto's.

The stains spread pink through the fabric.

Kara cleans the cut at her own hairline.

Neither speaks.

In the mirror, Ruthye's armor comes apart quietly.

Her shoulders lower. Her mouth trembles once. She looks thirteen. Smaller than the sword at her back. Farther from home than she has ever been.

A tear slips down before she can stop it.

Ruthye turns her face away.

Kara reaches across and catches the tear with her thumb.

No consolation. No promise.

Her hand settles on Ruthye's shoulder.

Ruthye looks at her in the mirror.

For a moment, she leans into the hand.

Then the tracker pulses from the counter.

Ruthye straightens. The mask returns, imperfectly.

She rinses the cloth and folds it with precise corners.

Kara gives her shoulder one last squeeze.

They leave together.

**36. INT. INTERGALACTIC BUS - PASSENGER CARS - LATER**

Ruthye forgets to be severe.

She nods to a shell-backed passenger before stepping over his extended feelers. Waits for a gravity pilgrim to finish a bow that takes up the whole aisle. Turns Ruthye's exposed blue cake inward when a passing creature hisses at the color.

How do you know?

KARA (CONT'D)

Row nineteen.

**37. INT. INTERGALACTIC BUS - ROW NINETEEN - LATER**

**41. EXT./INT. ORBITAL DROP-SHELL - SPACE - CONTINUOUS**

**42. EXT. BILKI DROP FIELD - DAY**

Kara.

Now.

Public standards.

**55. EXT. WRECKED DEPOT / SHUTTLE ROW - DAY**

Krem limps through the open bay of a Brigand shuttle.

Its engines are already hot. Cargo lights flash over an obscene heap of gold bars, cut gems, sealed reliquaries -- a survivor's fortune made entirely of other people's homes.

He dumps another sack into the hold.

Ruthye's father's white-gripped field pistol lies among the spill.

Krem kneels beside a dead Brigand and tears a poison-bolt launcher from the body armor. He locks it over one forearm.

On the other, he clamps an energy cannon into the suit ports. The weapon is too large for him. Its charge indicator jumps into red and stays there.

The armored antidote vial knocks against his sternum as he works.

A SONIC CRACK rolls across the row.

Krem looks up.

Kara lands behind a collapsed loading wall and sets Ruthye down.

KARA

Stay here.

RUTHYE

You said together.

KARA

We came back together.

RUTHYE

That is not the same.

Kara looks through the wall toward Krem. Her eyes give off the faintest red at the edges.

She makes them go dark.

KARA

No. It isn't.

RUTHYE

Then let me come.

KARA

I don't trust what I want right now.

That silences Ruthye.

Kara steps out onto the apron.

KARA (CONT'D)

Stay where I can keep you safe.

Ruthye's face hardens at the old instruction.

Kara walks toward the shuttle.


**56. EXT. SHUTTLE APRON - CONTINUOUS**

Krem comes out to meet her.

The energy cannon hums on one arm. Poison launcher on the other. His eyes are swollen from begging.

He looks smaller under a sky Kara has split open.

KREM

Look around you.

Kara keeps walking.

KREM (CONT'D)

There was never anyone to save us.

KARA

I believe you.

KREM

Then you know.

KARA

I know what you chose. Give me the antidote.

KREM

For the animal.

KARA

For my family.

Krem raises the poison launcher.

He fires.


**57. EXT. SHUTTLE APRON - CONTINUOUS**

The poison bolt crawls through the air.

To Kara, its shaft turns lazily. A bead of toxin gathers at its tip.

She plucks it out of flight.

For one beat she only looks at it -- the thing that stopped Krypto's heart one fraction at a time.

Her fingers close.

The bolt powders in her fist.

Krem fires again.

Kara is already in front of him.

She tears the launcher from his forearm and strikes him away.

The impact is not a punch. Barely a shove.

It breaks his arm and sends him across the apron into a stack of cargo cases. The shock wave bursts locks and blows loose jewels across the concrete.

Krem's scream arrives after he lands.

Kara stares at her own hand.

She had forgotten how little force the world can bear.

Krem tries to rise. Cannot.

Kara looks at the armored vial on his chest.

Then she looks at him.

Her eyes fill with heat.

Red light paints Krem's terrified face.

Behind the loading wall --

RUTHYE

Kara.

Only her name.

Kara's jaw tightens. The light trembles.

Then cools.

She presses two fingers to her temple, overwhelmed by the sound of Krem's racing heart, the engines, every little fire in the yard.

KARA

Listen to me. I only want the antidote.

Krem looks at her restraint and mistakes it for an opening.

His free hand finds the energy cannon's overload stud.


**58. EXT. SHUTTLE APRON - CONTINUOUS**

Krem slams the stud.

The cannon's charge vaults past its limit.

Kara hears the rising frequency. Turns.

He fires toward Kara and the loading wall behind her.

Kara moves between the blast and Ruthye.

The world goes red.

Then white.

A column of energy rips across the apron and bends skyward around Kara's body. The recoil tears the cannon apart on Krem's arm.

The explosion scatters the shuttle's cargo. Gold bars tumble through smoke. Gems strike concrete like bright rain. The white-gripped pistol spins beneath a landing strut.

Kara is hurled through two cargo frames and vanishes into the wreckage beyond.

Not wounded. Gone from sight.

The loading wall shields Ruthye from the debris. She lifts her head into ringing silence.

Through the smoke, the tracker flashes in her hand.

The white weapon icon is almost on top of her.

Beyond it, Krem lies on his back.

The ruined cannon smolders on one arm. The other is bent beneath him. His clothes are burned, his face bloodless.

The armored vial remains intact against his chest.

Ruthye steps out.


**59. EXT. SHUTTLE APRON - CONTINUOUS**

Ruthye crosses the field of stolen wealth.

Her tracker screams proximity.

She finds the white-gripped pistol under the landing strut and picks it up. Ash has worked into the engraving, but the grip is clean.

Her father's hand fits over hers in memory.

Then it is only a weapon.

She tucks it into her belt.

Krem sees her coming.

He does not crawl. There is nowhere left in him to go.

Ruthye draws her sword.

Krem looks at the blade.

KREM

Just do it.

No lesson. No dare.

Ruthye stops over him.

Krem's eyes close.

The armored chain moves against the pulse in his throat. One stroke could reach either.

A dark line of Kara's dried blood remains near the hilt of Ruthye's sword. Ruthye sees it. Her grip does not loosen.

Behind her, ash stirs.

Kara lands within the fading smoke.

Only breaths have passed.

She sees the position of Ruthye's feet. The tension in both hands around the sword. The chain across Krem's throat.

She could cross the distance before Ruthye's next heartbeat.

Kara does not call Ruthye's name.

Kara takes one step.

Stops.

Her fists open at her sides.

Ruthye draws one slow breath.

Ruthye raises the sword.


**60. EXT. SHUTTLE APRON - CONTINUOUS**

The blade falls.

Metal rings against metal.

The sword has cut the chain.

The armored vial lifts on the flat of Ruthye's blade. She catches it in her hand.

Ruthye turns away from Krem.

We stay with Ruthye.

Kara stands in the smoke, relief taking the strength out of her face.

Neither of them looks back.

Ruthye walks to Kara and holds out the vial.

Kara closes Ruthye's fingers around it instead.

KARA

You carry it.

Kara pulls Ruthye into a fierce, brief embrace. Ruthye holds on with one hand and keeps the antidote safe in the other.

RUTHYE

Let's go home.

They run for the warmed shuttle.


**61. INT./EXT. BRIGAND SHUTTLE - MOMENTS LATER**

The cargo ramp rises behind them.

Kara drops into the pilot's seat. The controls are built for someone with more fingers and fewer objections. She learns them at a glance.

Ruthye straps in beside her, armored vial locked between both hands. Her father's pistol rests across her knees.

KARA

Seal?

Ruthye checks the vial. A tiny green line runs unbroken around its casing.

RUTHYE

Intact.

Kara punches the launch control.

The shuttle claws away from the apron.

The ground drops away and is gone.

On Ruthye's tracker, the weapon icon stays fixed at her own position. The needle spins uselessly, searching for a destination it is already holding.

Ruthye looks at the white grip.

RUTHYE

He said a tool should know where home is.

She switches the tracker off.

The cabin grows quieter.

Kara opens a priority channel while she drives them through the torn cloud deck.

KARA

Rest-stop dock clinic, this is the vessel attached to your canine patient. We have the antidote. Prep an injection and keep the tube running.

Static. Then the HEALER, thin with distance.

HEALER (V.O.)

Seal mark?

Ruthye reads the symbols on the casing.

RUTHYE

Black crescent. Three vertical cuts.

HEALER (V.O.)

Correct series. Do not open it in transit.

KARA

How is he?

A beat too long.

HEALER (V.O.)

Come quickly.

The channel ends.

Kara lays in the jump coordinates.

The shuttle computer objects in a language neither of them understands.

KARA

That sounded optional.

RUTHYE

It sounded profane.

KARA

Also optional.

Kara drives the throttle forward.

Bilki falls behind. The yellow sun fills the windshield, then contracts into the jump aperture.

Ruthye watches Kara's hands on the controls.

RUTHYE

I am sorry.

KARA

I know.

RUTHYE

No. You know I did not mean to cut you. That is not the whole apology.

Kara keeps her eyes on the aperture.

RUTHYE (CONT'D)

I meant to swing.

KARA

I know that too.

RUTHYE

You should not have had to step in front of me.

KARA

You should not have had to watch your father die.

RUTHYE

Those things are not equal.

KARA

No. They are both true.

The jump drive reaches pitch.

Ruthye looks down at the vial.

RUTHYE

I wanted to kill him until the sword was in my hands.

Kara waits.

RUTHYE (CONT'D)

Then I wanted to want it.

KARA

And now?

RUTHYE

Now Krypto is waiting.

Kara looks over at her.

KARA

That's enough.

RUTHYE

Are you still angry?

KARA

Yes.

RUTHYE

At me?

KARA

At him. At myself. At everyone who could have helped his world and didn't.

The shuttle jumps.


**62. INT. REST STOP - DOCK CLINIC - LATER**

Clinic doors fly open.

Kara rushes in at human speed because the room is crowded and fragile. Ruthye keeps pace beside her, holding the vial.

Krypto floats in the stabilizing tube.

He is completely still.

The HEALER waits with an injector cradle and a cutting tool.

HEALER

Here.

Ruthye sets the armored vial into the cradle.

Clamps close. The tool slices the casing along its seal. A glass ampule glows inside, clear fluid around one thread of black.

HEALER (CONT'D)

Authentic.

KARA

Do it.

The Healer draws the fluid. Fits the injector to a port at Krypto's chest.

Kara presses both hands to the tube.

KARA (CONT'D)

Come on, buddy.

The injector fires.

Nothing.

The black thread moves beneath Krypto's skin, up his neck, then dissolves.

Still nothing.

Ruthye stands at Kara's shoulder.

RUTHYE

Come on, Krypto.

Kara nods once.

KARA

Come on.

Krypto's heart gives one dull beat.

Kara hears it before the monitor does.

Her face changes.

Another beat.

Krypto drags in a breath. The liquid in the tube churns around him.

His eyes open.

KARA (CONT'D)

Hey.

The tube drains. Its front unfolds.

Krypto tries to stand, slips, and tumbles bodily into Kara. She catches him on her knees.

He licks her face with desperate, furious devotion.

Kara laughs into his fur and cries where nobody can tell the difference.

KARA (CONT'D)

I know. I know. I came back.

Krypto sees Ruthye.

His ears lift. He leans out of Kara's arms and presses his nose to Ruthye's bloody sleeve.

Ruthye touches his head.

RUTHYE

You were very inconvenient.

Krypto licks her hand.

Ruthye's face crumples. Kara reaches up and pulls her into the heap.

The monitor settles into a slow, steady rhythm.

Kara holds girl and dog tighter. The steady rhythm fills the room.


**63. INT. REST STOP - MECHANIC VAT BAY - LATER**

Krypto sleeps under a thermal wrap on a rolling cart, his tail giving an occasional healthy thump.

The MECHANIC stands beneath the Brigand shuttle with a diagnostic wand.

Kara and Ruthye wait beside Kara's powerless ship.

MECHANIC

You left with two bus tickets.

The Mechanic looks up at the military shuttle.

MECHANIC (CONT'D)

You came back with a donor.

KARA

Can you use it?

MECHANIC

Its jump-seed is military-bred. Vicious little organism. It'll eat your dead drive root and call it breakfast.

RUTHYE

Is that good?

MECHANIC

For leaving, yes. For a long and emotionally stable relationship with your engine, no.

The Mechanic reaches into an access cavity. A caged crystal mass retracts from the shuttle in a web of living silver fibers.

It snaps at the diagnostic wand.

MECHANIC (CONT'D)

See? Rude.

KARA

How long?

MECHANIC

Graft takes twelve minutes. It will make your ship viable. Maybe not polite.

KARA

Viable is all I need.

MECHANIC

The donor hull settles the clinic, dock, and repair liens. You leave with your ship, not both.

KARA

Keep it.

Ruthye looks between the ships.

RUTHYE

The shuttle was stolen.

MECHANIC

Then stolen again. That cancels out in three transit jurisdictions.

RUTHYE

There are seven jurisdictions here.

The Mechanic points the wand at her.

MECHANIC

Never read the small ring of print.

The jump-seed is wheeled toward Kara's ship. It strains in its cage like a bad-tempered star.

Krypto raises his head and growls at it.

KARA

Oh, good. You two agree.

The seed growls back.

For the first time, the road ahead is ordinary enough to be funny.


**64. EXT. KNOLL HOMESTEAD - RED EVENING**

Kara's repaired ship settles into the field.

The grafted drive winds down with an irritable animal CLICKING beneath the hull.

The homestead stands where they left it. One kitchen window has been boarded. Father's cairn rises on the knoll beyond the house.

MOTHER and TAVI wait at the edge of the field.

Ruthye appears in the open hatch.

For all the worlds she crossed, the last few steps stop her.

Tavi runs toward her.

Halfway there, he stops.

His small fists knot at his sides.

TAVI

You left.

Ruthye comes down the ramp.

RUTHYE

I know.

TAVI

Mother said you would come back.

MOTHER

I said I needed her to.

Mother does not take her eyes off Ruthye.

Ruthye reaches the ground.

MOTHER (CONT'D)

I told you not to go.

RUTHYE

I know.

MOTHER

I had one grave on the hill.

Her voice nearly fails.

MOTHER (CONT'D)

You made me stand here and imagine two.

Ruthye's composure gives way.

RUTHYE

I am sorry.

Mother crosses the remaining distance and takes Ruthye's face in both hands. She examines every bruise, every cut, as if counting a child back into existence.

RUTHYE (CONT'D)

I came home.

MOTHER

You did.

Mother pulls her close.

MOTHER (CONT'D)

Coming home does not make him less dead.

Ruthye grips the back of Mother's coat.

RUTHYE

I know.

MOTHER

It does not make what you did to us disappear.

RUTHYE

I know.

MOTHER

Good.

Mother holds her tighter.

MOTHER (CONT'D)

Then we can begin with what is true.

Ruthye folds into her.

Tavi stays where he is.

Ruthye sees him over Mother's shoulder. She lowers herself to his height.

TAVI

Did you kill him?

Ruthye does not look away.

RUTHYE

No.

TAVI

Then why did you go?

RUTHYE

I thought if I killed him, what happened would have an end.

TAVI

Did it?

RUTHYE

No.

TAVI

So it was for nothing.

Ruthye takes that blow.

RUTHYE

I learned there was something I wanted more.

TAVI

What?

RUTHYE

You.

Tavi's mouth twists.

TAVI

I was already here.

RUTHYE

I know.

She begins to cry.

RUTHYE (CONT'D)

I am sorry I did not understand that.

Tavi launches into her. Ruthye holds him against her, the way she did in the kitchen, but her eyes are closed now. Her face is buried in his hair.

Mother kneels around both children.

Ruthye opens her weathered satchel and takes out Tavi's paper kite. One wing is crushed, but it is whole.

RUTHYE

It survived the road.

Tavi takes it carefully.

TAVI

You can fix it.

RUTHYE

We can.

Kara remains in the ship's shadow. Krypto leans against her leg, still weak but upright.

Ruthye takes the white-gripped pistol from her belt.

RUTHYE (CONT'D)

I brought Father's pistol back.

She offers it to Mother.

Mother looks at the weapon. Then pushes Ruthye's hand gently down.

MOTHER

You are not a tool that has to earn its way home.

Ruthye looks toward the cairn.

She carries the pistol up the first few steps of the knoll and lays it at the base of the stones.

RUTHYE

It knows now.

Wind moves through the field.

Krypto gives a small BARK.

Tavi notices him.

TAVI

Is that the poisoned dog?

Krypto looks offended.

KARA

Formerly poisoned.

Tavi approaches with great care. Krypto meets him halfway and puts his head under the boy's hand.

Tavi scratches behind one ear.

TAVI

He's warm.

KARA

Yeah.

Mother looks at Kara.

MOTHER

You took my daughter off-world.

KARA

I tried very hard not to.

RUTHYE

She told me to come home.

MOTHER

Not hard enough, apparently.

KARA

Apparently.

Ruthye comes back down from the cairn.

RUTHYE

She saved my life.

Kara looks at Ruthye.

KARA

And then she saved mine.

Mother studies both of them. The blood has been washed away, but she sees what remains.

At last, she turns toward the house.

MOTHER

There is food.

An offer. Freely made.

Kara looks through the kitchen window at the table.

Then follows the family inside.


**65. INT. HOMESTEAD KITCHEN - RED EVENING**

The table has been scrubbed. One scar in the wood will not come out.

Father's chair is empty.

No one has moved it.

Mother sets a fresh pie at the center of the table.

Kara stands behind the last open chair, uncertain.

MOTHER

Sit.

Kara looks at the pie.

Mother understands.

MOTHER (CONT'D)

This one is offered.

TAVI

We only had six.

KARA

That's exactly how old I am.

Tavi gives her a flat look.

TAVI

No, it isn't.

KARA

Worth a try.

He pushes the candles into the pie. Mother lights them.

Ruthye sits beside Tavi. She keeps one hand near his, not quite holding it until he turns his palm over and lets her.

Krypto lies under the table with his muzzle on Kara's boot.

TAVI

Father made that crust.

The room stills.

MOTHER

He taught us the crust.

TAVI

He always burned the edge.

RUTHYE

He said the burned part had more character.

MOTHER

He lied about many small things.

A fragile smile passes between them.

Then she serves the living.

Kara looks at the six flames.

RUTHYE

Do you make wishes on Earth?

KARA

Sometimes.

RUTHYE

Do they happen?

Kara looks around the table. At Mother. Tavi. Ruthye. Krypto.

KARA

Not the way you ask.

She closes her eyes and blows gently.

Five candles go out.

The sixth bends almost horizontal, stubbornly lit.

Tavi leans over and pinches it out with wet fingers.

TAVI

Now it counts.

KARA

Thank you.

They eat.

Kara takes one bite and stops.

KARA (CONT'D)

Okay. The burned edge was not the problem.

Mother almost laughs.

Under the table, Krypto's eyes open.

His nose rises toward the pie.

Kara feels the movement with her boot.

KARA (CONT'D)

Don't.

Krypto freezes.

Tavi quietly lowers a piece of crust below the table.

KARA (CONT'D)

Also don't.

Tavi freezes.

Ruthye lowers her own crust from the other side.

KARA (CONT'D)

I can hear both of you.

He rockets out from under the table, catches both pieces, and slides across the floor into a basket of folded linens.

Tavi laughs.

Ruthye's face opens in a wide smile, but the sound stays caught behind it.

Kara points at Krypto.

KARA (CONT'D)

You have a doctor's note.

MOTHER

It seems to have improved his appetite.

Kara retrieves the basket, dog and all, and brings him back beside her chair.

For a little while, they eat pie in a room where something terrible happened.

The red sun rests on the horizon. Kara's ship waits in the field, repaired hull glowing in the living evening.

Kara stands at the foot of the ramp with Krypto beside her.

Ruthye faces her. Mother and Tavi wait a few paces back.

RUTHYE

I wish I could go with you.

RUTHYE (CONT'D)

This is where you belong.

KARA (CONT'D)

On the other hand, six candles barely qualifies as a birthday.

You said six was exactly right.

I was trying to be a gracious guest.

KARA (CONT'D)

I'm working on it.

KARA (CONT'D)

One short flight. We never finished celebrating.

One circuit of the lower valley.

KARA

One circuit.

The lower valley.

KARA

Very specifically that valley.

MOTHER

Before the night bell.

KARA

Before the bell.

TAVI

I want to fly.

RUTHYE

Next time.

Birthday control.

**68. INT. METROPOLIS APARTMENT - NIGHT**

A city of yellow windows stretches to the horizon.

Kara stands at the glass in clean clothes, hands empty. Her reflection is sad, awake, present.

No bottle. No dead sun. Nothing between her and the feeling.

KARA (CONT'D)

Don't make a thing out of it.

Home.
