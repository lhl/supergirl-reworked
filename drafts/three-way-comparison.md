# Three-way draft comparison — GPT vs Opus vs Fable (objective)

Purpose: a **descriptive, non-evaluative** map of how the three drafts solve the same story — characterizations, scenes, and staged action — so choices can be redlined by hand. No ranking, no "stronger/weaker." Where a draft is quoted, it's to show a concrete difference, not to endorse it.

Drafts covered: `draft-script-gpt5.5.md`, `draft-script-opus.md`, `draft-script-fable.md`. (`glm5.2` out of scope here.)

## 0. Register / form (observable, not a judgment)

How each draft writes the *page*, which affects how much converts cleanly to a shooting script:

| | Action-line style | Authorial voice in action lines | Dialogue density |
|---|---|---|---|
| **gpt** | lean, sparse, present-tense beats; near-shooting-script | minimal; rarely interprets | high; short exchanges carry scenes |
| **opus** | descriptive, image-forward; some interpretation | occasional ("we watch him decide") | medium; dialogue + atmosphere share load |
| **fable** | long, essayistic; frequently addresses the reader/audience and states intent ("this is the thesis," "the audience holds its breath," "the film keeps its own counsel") | heavy; interprets its own scenes | medium; wrapped in interpretive prose |

Consequence for merging: fable's *intent* is explicit on the page, but much of what reads as weight is narration that won't shoot and must be cut or converted. gpt is closest to a usable script surface; opus sits between.

## 3. Deep dives (the scenes that diverge most)

### 3.1 Cold open / Krem

Same skeleton in all three: ordinary warm family → polite (non-violent) entrance → Krem takes everything → an *off-ramp exists* → he chooses to kill the father as a lesson, eyes on the child. Divergences:

- **What Krem carries out of the scene as an object.** gpt/opus: nothing specific. **fable: the father's hunting crossbow**, which he then kills with — so the murder weapon, the tracked object, the brawl weapon, and the climax object are one thing. This is fable's biggest structural choice in the cold open.
- **Weapon of the kill.** gpt: unspecified gun. opus: a short **blade**, kneeling by the pinned father. fable: the crossbow.
- **How the mirror is planted.** fable names **Bilki** and the **Yellow Hills** here, so the destination and Krem's origin are seeded in scene 1. gpt/opus keep his origin generic (no place name).
- **The mother's blow.** opus and fable both stage a backhand to the mother "to see who moves" / "procedure." gpt has it too but less foregrounded as a test.
- **Family texture.** gpt: Father/Mother/Brother (unnamed). opus: Alder/Nell/Pip. fable: Andy/Marye/Tomme, plus a six-legged pet and market/dance banter.

### 3.2 Medbay

Same function (diagnose poison, establish no on-hand cure, decide to run for a yellow sun). The three "solve" the dread differently:

- **gpt:** shows **numbers** ("not friendly"), then undercuts with a human detail — a **child's drawing of Krypto** taped over Kryptonian instructions ("Someone who thought I answered messages") — and gives Ruthye a **hands-on mechanical task** (the pressure valve; "And if you do not say?" / "Use judgment"), establishing her competence early.
- **opus:** replaces numbers with a **metaphor** — the toxicology readout is a **decay curve**, and Ruthye reads it: "It's not a clock. It's a hill." Kara explains the sun as "a plant that's been in a closet." No drawing, no valve; the weight is in the image.
- **fable:** replaces numbers with **sound** — a medbay **tick** ("a metronome the movie will never let us stop hearing") and "It gives him 'soon.'" Ends on a gut-punch question — "**why in all the worlds do you live here?**" — that Kara can't answer. Ruthye is "present," not mechanical.

### 3.3 Rest stop (mechanic + healer + info desk)

Same three transactions: (a) the ship needs a slow **vat-grown** part; (b) the poison has **one antidote, held only by ranking Brigands**; (c) the tracker points to **Bilki**, reachable only by a no-return drop. Divergences are mostly **tone and the length of the clock**:

- **Mechanic clock:** gpt "days" · opus "nine days" · fable "two weeks." (Sets how hard the ship-vs-dog squeeze reads.)
- **Mechanic comedy:** gpt runs a bit ("I'll restrain it emotionally"); opus plays Vesk as slow-as-soup ("the vat has its own opinions"); fable is deadpan-insult ("you asked it to die and it agreed" / "It's a QUALITY VAT").
- **Healer's framing of the squeeze:** opus states it as a thesis line ("Your ship needs longer to heal than your dog has"); gpt/fable imply it.
- **The stasis-tube image:** fable makes it a **pod-window echo** (paw against glass, "glass between them"); opus flattens the decay-curve on the tube; gpt keeps it functional.
- **Station identity:** unnamed (gpt) vs Meridian (opus) vs Oasis (fable) — a naming/branding choice, plus opus/fable each give the concourse a distinct comedic palette.

## 4. Signature differences, at a glance (objective)

- **Object logic:** only **fable** threads a single physical object (the crossbow) through cold-open → tracker → brawl → climax.
- **Dread device:** gpt = numbers; opus = the "hill"; fable = the "tick." (Three different non-interchangeable choices for the same clock.)
- **Ruthye's early agency:** gpt gives her mechanical competence (valve, turret hinge); opus/fable keep her more observer-until-late.
- **Clark:** opus alone stages him in person at the end.
- **Backstory delivery:** gpt states the moral rule outright on the bus; opus leads with imagery ("the quiet after"); fable structures it as sanitized-telling now / full-showing later.
- **Krem's kill:** gun (gpt) vs blade (opus) vs the father's own crossbow (fable).
- **Repair clock length:** days / nine days / two weeks.
- **Register:** gpt reads as script; fable reads as annotated treatment; opus between.
